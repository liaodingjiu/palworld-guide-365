🧬 How Breeding Works

Palworld's breeding system is deterministic, not random. Once you understand the hidden breeding power mechanic, you can reliably produce any Pal you want.

The Breeding Power System

Every Pal in Palworld has a hidden numerical value called breeding power. This number is not shown anywhere in the game UI — it is an internal data value assigned by the developers. When you breed two Pals together, the game calculates the average of both parents' breeding power values and then finds the Pal species whose breeding power is closest to that average. The offspring will always be that specific Pal.

Because this system is mathematically deterministic, the same two parent species will always produce the same child species. There is no RNG involved in determining which species is born — only in which passive skills and IVs are inherited. This is why breeding charts and calculators can predict every single combination in the game with 100% accuracy.

As a general rule, lower breeding power values correspond to rarer and more powerful Pals. Common early-game Pals like Lamball have high breeding power values (around 1500), while endgame Legendaries like Jetragon have very low values (around 10). When you breed a high-power Pal with a low-power Pal, the average lands somewhere in the middle, producing a mid-tier Pal. This is why you cannot simply breed a Lamball with a Jetragon to get another Jetragon — the average of their breeding powers will produce something in between.

Key Concept: Breeding power is species-based, not individual-based. Every Lamball has the same breeding power. Every Jetragon has the same breeding power. The only things that vary between individuals of the same species are passive skills and IVs — not breeding power.

Pairing Mechanics

To breed two Pals, you need exactly one male and one female. The gender of the parents does not affect the offspring species — only the breeding power average matters. However, the offspring's gender is randomized, which matters if you plan to chain-breed (more on that in the chain breeding section below).

Some Pals are gender-locked — for example, Frostallion is always female and Jetragon has no gender at all. Genderless Pals cannot be bred, which means Jetragon, Paladius, Necromus, and a few others are excluded from the breeding system entirely. You can only obtain them by catching them in the wild.

Cake Recipe

Breeding requires Cake. Without a Cake placed in the Breeding Farm's cake box, no egg will be produced — the assigned Pals will simply wander around doing nothing. One Cake is consumed per breeding cycle, but a single Cake actually lasts for multiple breedings before disappearing. Here is the exact recipe:

IngredientQuantityHow to Obtain
Flour×5Mill Wheat → Flour (need Wheat Plantation + Mill)
Red Berries×8Berry Plantation or wild bushes
Milk×7Mozzarina at the Ranch (auto-produces Milk)
Egg×8Chikipi at the Ranch (auto-produces Eggs)
Honey×2Elizabee at the Ranch, or defeat/beat wild Elizabee

The Cake must be cooked at a Cooking Pot using a Pal with the Kindling work suitability (e.g., Foxparks, Jormuntide Ignis). It takes about 30 seconds of in-game cooking time. Because the ingredients require a functioning farm ecosystem — Wheat Plantation, Berry Plantation, Ranch with Mozzarina, Chikipi, and Elizabee — setting up Cake production is one of the first major milestones for any serious breeder.

Production Tip: Assign 3–4 Mozzarinas and 3–4 Chikipis to your Ranch to maintain a steady supply of Milk and Eggs. Build a Wheat Plantation early — Flour is the bottleneck ingredient since Wheat takes time to grow and the Mill processes it slowly.

Breeding Farm Unlock

The Breeding Farm is unlocked at Character Level 19 in the Technology tree. It costs 2 Technology Points to unlock and requires the following materials to construct:

  • Wood ×100 — Harvest from trees with an axe or assign Lumbering Pals
  • Stone ×20 — Mine rocks with a pickaxe or assign Mining Pals
  • Fiber ×50 — Harvest from small bushes or assign Gathering Pals

The Breeding Farm occupies a 2×2 area and must be placed within your base's build radius. Once built, it has a cake box on the side where you deposit Cake, and a central pen where the assigned Pals roam. You can assign Pals by picking them up (default key: F) and throwing them at the farm — just like assigning Pals to any other base structure.

📋 Breeding Basics: Step by Step

From unlocking the farm to hatching your first bred Pal — here is the complete breeding workflow.

Step 1 — Reach Level 19 and Unlock the Farm

Grind experience by catching Pals (the first 10 catches of each species grant bonus XP), clearing dungeons, and defeating Tower Bosses. Once you hit Level 19, open the Technology menu and spend 2 points to unlock the Breeding Farm. Build it at your base using 100 Wood, 20 Stone, and 50 Fiber.

Step 2 — Set Up Cake Production

Before you can breed, you need Cake. Set up a Wheat Plantation and a Berry Plantation at your base. Build a Ranch and assign Mozzarinas (for Milk), Chikipis (for Eggs), and Elizabees (for Honey). Once you have the ingredients, cook a Cake at a Cooking Pot using a Kindling Pal.

Efficiency Tip: Build your Wheat Plantation near a Mill so your Transporting Pals can move Wheat to the Mill automatically. Assign a Pal with Watering Lv3+ (like Jormuntide) to keep the plantation irrigated, and a Pal with Planting Lv3+ (like Lyleen) to handle planting.

Step 3 — Assign Your Breeding Pair

Decide which Pal you want to breed. Look up the breeding combination on our combo table below or use a breeding calculator. Catch or breed the two parent Pals you need. Pick up one male and one female, and throw them into the Breeding Farm. They will be assigned automatically.

Step 4 — Place the Cake and Wait

Walk up to the Breeding Farm and interact with the cake box. Deposit your Cake. A progress bar will appear above the farm, slowly filling over approximately 5 minutes of real time. When the bar is full, an egg will appear on the ground near the farm.

Step 5 — Incubate and Hatch

Build an Egg Incubator (unlocked at Level 7 in the Technology tree, requires 10 Paldium Fragments, 5 Cloth, and 2 Ancient Civilization Parts). Pick up the egg and place it in the incubator. Incubation time varies by egg size and world settings — on default settings, a regular egg takes about 20 minutes, while a huge egg can take up to 2 hours. You can speed this up with the "Incubation Speed" server setting or by placing the incubator in an optimally heated/cooled environment.

Hatching Tip: Egg incubators have a temperature gauge. Each egg type has an ideal temperature range. If the incubator says "Seems very comfortable," the egg hatches at maximum speed. Use campfires, heaters, or coolers to adjust the ambient temperature around the incubator for faster hatching.

Step 6 — Check the Result and Repeat

Once hatched, check your new Pal's passive skills and IVs. If it doesn't have the passives you want, breed again — each breeding cycle is independent, and passive inheritance has an element of randomness. With Cake production automated, you can run continuous breeding loops until you get the perfect Pal.

✨ Passive Skill Inheritance

Passive skills are the most important factor in breeding for power. Here's how to pass down the best passives to your bred Pals.

Each Pal can have up to 4 passive skills simultaneously. These passives significantly alter the Pal's performance — some boost attack by 30%, others increase work speed by 50%, and a few provide defensive bonuses. When you breed two Pals, the offspring has a chance to inherit passives from either parent. The inheritance system works as follows:

  • The child is born with 0–4 passive skill slots filled.
  • Each slot is rolled independently — the game randomly picks a passive from the combined pool of both parents' passives.
  • There is also a small chance of a random passive being generated that neither parent has, but this is relatively rare.
  • If both parents share the same passive, the child has a significantly higher chance of inheriting it.

To breed a Pal with the perfect passive set — for example, an Anubis with Artisan, Serious, Work Slave, and Lucky — you need to follow a multi-generational approach. Start by catching multiple Penking and Bushi with at least one desirable passive each. Breed them together. If the child inherits a good passive, breed that child with another parent that has a different good passive. Over several generations, you can stack all four desired passives onto a single Pal.

Stacking Strategy: Breed two parents that each have 2 different desirable passives. If the child inherits all 4, you are done. If it inherits 3, breed it with a parent that has the 4th passive. The probability of inheriting a shared passive is much higher, so try to get both parents to share at least one passive before going for the final roll.

📊 IV System Explained

Individual Values (IVs) are hidden stat multipliers that determine how strong your Pal can become at max level. Here's how they work and how breeding affects them.

What Are IVs?

Every Pal in Palworld has three hidden IV stats: HP IV, Attack IV, and Defense IV. Each IV ranges from 0 to 100 and acts as a percentage multiplier on the Pal's base stats at max level. A Pal with 100 HP IV will have significantly more health than the same species with 0 HP IV at Level 50.

IVs are not shown in the game UI — you can only check them using external IV calculators or the Pal Inspection mod. This makes them easy to ignore for casual players, but for competitive breeding and endgame optimization, IVs are the difference between a good Pal and a perfect Pal.

How Breeding Affects IVs

When two Pals are bred, the child's IVs are determined by averaging the parents' IVs with a random variance. Specifically, for each of the three IV stats (HP, ATK, DEF), the child's value is calculated as:

Child IV = (Parent 1 IV + Parent 2 IV) / 2 ± Random Variance

The random variance is typically small (around ±10 points), which means breeding two high-IV parents reliably produces a child with high IVs. By breeding generation after generation and always selecting the highest-IV offspring as the next parent, you can gradually push all three IVs toward 100 — a process called IV breeding.

Condensing for IV Boosts

Beyond breeding, you can also increase a Pal's IVs through the Pal Condensation system at the Statue of Power. By sacrificing duplicate Pals of the same species (condensing them into the target Pal), you permanently increase the target's IVs. Each condensation tier adds a flat bonus to all three IV stats. Condensing 4 duplicates reaches the first bonus tier; condensing 116 total duplicates maxes out the condensation gauge for the maximum IV bonus.

Optimization Tip: Combine breeding with condensation for the best results. First, breed two high-IV parents to get a child with strong base IVs. Then, condense surplus duplicates of the same species into that child to push IVs even higher. This hybrid approach saves time compared to relying on either method alone.

🔗 Chain Breeding Strategy

Sometimes you can't breed your target Pal directly because you don't have the required parents. Chain breeding solves this by producing intermediate Pals step by step.

What Is Chain Breeding?

Chain breeding is the process of breeding through multiple generations to eventually produce a target Pal that you cannot breed directly. This happens when the parents required for your target are themselves difficult to obtain or are breeding-exclusive Pals.

For example, suppose you want to breed Shadowbeak, which requires Kitsun + Astegon. You catch a Kitsun easily, but you don't have an Astegon. Astegon itself requires Helzephyr + Cryolinx. You don't have a Cryolinx either, so you look up Cryolinx's breeding combo — and so on. By planning the full breeding chain in reverse, from your target back to Pals you can catch in the wild, you can reach any breeding-exclusive Pal in the game.

How to Plan a Breeding Chain

  1. Identify your target Pal and look up its breeding combination.
  2. For each parent, check whether you can catch it in the wild. If yes, go catch it. If no, look up its breeding combination too.
  3. Continue tracing backwards until every branch of the tree terminates at a Pal you can catch in the wild.
  4. Work forward from the wild-caught Pals, breeding each intermediate step until you reach your target.
  5. Keep track of gender — you need one male and one female for each breeding step. If you get the wrong gender, breed again or catch more.

Once you've bred your perfect Pal, check our Best Pals tier list to see how it stacks up, and head to our Tower Boss guide for strategies on using your bred Pals in combat.

Even if your chain fails to produce the right gender for the next step, the intermediate Pals are still valuable additions to your base.

Gender Management

One of the most frustrating aspects of chain breeding is gender management. Each breeding step requires one male and one female, but the offspring's gender is random (roughly 50/50). This means you may need to breed the same combination multiple times to get the gender you need for the next step in the chain.

To minimize frustration, breed 2–3 eggs at each step instead of just 1. This gives you a higher chance of getting both genders, and you can use the extras for condensation or as backup parents. If you get a surplus of one gender, you can always release them or use them in the Condensation system at the Statue of Power.
