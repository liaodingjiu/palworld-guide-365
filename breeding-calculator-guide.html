⚡ Quick Answer: What the Calculator Does

If you only read one section, read this one.

Our Palworld Breeding Calculator answers three questions instantly:

  1. What does A + B make? — Select any two Pals and see their offspring. The formula is pure math: every Pal has a hidden Breeding Power (90 for Jetragon to 1500 for Chikipi). The game averages the parents' values and picks the closest match. Our calculator does this instantly and shows you the exact math.
  2. Which parents make my target? — Reverse Search mode finds every possible parent pair for any Pal. Want an Anubis but don't know how? The calculator shows all combinations, sorted by closest Breeding Power match.
  3. What's the full breeding chain? — The Breeding Chain button visualizes the multi-generation tree: grandparents → parents → child. Useful when your target requires intermediate breeding steps.
Why the calculator works: Breeding in Palworld is 100% deterministic for species. The same two parents always produce the same child. There is zero RNG on species — only on passives, IVs, and gender. The calculator isn't guessing; it's doing the exact Breeding Power math the game uses internally.

🧬 How to Use the Calculator — All 3 Modes

Our Breeding Calculator supports three core workflows. Here's exactly how to use each one.

Mode 1: Find the Offspring (Parents → Child)

Use when: You have two Pals and want to know what they'll produce.

  1. Open the calculator — go to our Breeding Calculator.
  2. Click a Pal card to select Parent A — or use the search bar to filter by name. The selected Pal appears in the left box with its Breeding Power displayed.
  3. Click another Pal card to select Parent B — it fills the right box.
  4. Read the result — the child appears instantly in the center box, with the exact Breeding Power formula shown below: (BP₁ + BP₂) ÷ 2 = avg → closest Pal.
Parent order does not matter. Anubis + Frostallion = Frostallion + Anubis. The game averages Breeding Power, and average is commutative. If any calculator shows different results for swapped parents, it's bugged.

Mode 2: Reverse Search (Find All Parents for a Target)

Use when: You want a specific Pal (e.g., Anubis) and need to know which parents can produce it.

  1. Click the "🔍 Find All Combinations for a Pal" button at the top of the calculator.
  2. Search or browse for your target Pal in the modal that opens.
  3. Click the Pal — the calculator instantly shows every valid parent pair, sorted by closest Breeding Power average (most reliable combos at the top).
  4. Switch tabs to "As a Parent" to see what offspring this Pal produces with every other Pal.

For popular targets like Anubis, you'll see 15–30+ possible parent combinations. Scroll through and pick the pair where you already own both Pals — or where both are easy to catch at your current level.

Mode 3: Breeding Chain (Multi-Generation Tree)

Use when: Your target requires parents you don't own — and those parents also need breeding.

  1. Select your target's parents in the main calculator (use Mode 2 to find them first).
  2. Click "🌳 View Breeding Chain" — the calculator generates a 3-generation tree showing grandparents, parents, and child.
  3. Work bottom-up — breed the grandparents first, then use their offspring as parents for the next generation.
Example: Anubis breeding chain. Select Penking + Bushi → click "View Breeding Chain." The tree shows: you need Penking and Bushi (wild-caught) as parents, and Anubis as the child. If your target requires more steps, check our Breeding Combos page for 53 guaranteed special combinations — many of them are intermediate steps in longer chains.

Before You Start: The Pre-Flight Checklist

Minimum Requirements: If you haven't done these four things, the calculator can't help you yet.
  • ☑ Reach Level 19 — Unlocks the Breeding Farm in the Technology tree. Fastest path: catch 10 of each Pal species (bonus XP per first 10 catches).
  • ☑ Unlock the Breeding Farm — Costs 2 Technology Points. Build cost: Wood ×100, Stone ×20, Fiber ×50.
  • ☑ Build at least one Egg Incubator — Unlocked at Level 7 (Ancient Technology). Requires: Paldium Fragments ×10, Cloth ×5, Ancient Civilization Parts ×2. Build 3–4 so you can hatch eggs in parallel.
  • ☑ Automate Cake Production — Without Cake in the farm's chest, no egg appears. You need:
    • Wheat Plantation → Mill → Flour (×5 per Cake)
    • Berry Plantation → Red Berries (×8 per Cake)
    • Mozzarina at Ranch → Milk (×7 per Cake)
    • Chikipi at Ranch → Eggs (×8 per Cake)
    • Beegarde or Elizabee at Ranch → Honey (×2 per Cake)
    • Cooking Pot + Kindling Pal → cook into Cake

🏆 Beyond the Calculator: Breeding for Perfect Passives

The calculator tells you the species. Passives are your job — here's the strategy.

If you want a Pal with specific passive skills (e.g., Demon God + Serenity + Musclehead + Ferocious), you need to layer passive breeding on top of the calculator's species recipe:

  1. Use the calculator to get the right species first. Lock in the species, then work on passives.
  2. Capture wild Pals with desired passives. Legendary bosses always carry "Legend." Musclehead, Ferocious, and Serenity appear randomly on wild Pals. Demon God requires Special Cake (Mutation system).
  3. Breed parents that each carry 1–2 desired passives — fewer junk passives on each parent = less dilution in the offspring.
  4. Hatch eggs in batches. Passive inheritance is random. Expect 20–100+ eggs for a perfect 4-passive Pal.
  5. Use good offspring as new parents. When you get a Pal with 2–3 desired passives, swap it in to consolidate the gene pool.
  6. Repeat until all 4 passives land on the same Pal.
Passive inheritance odds (community estimate): Each passive on a parent has roughly a 40–60% chance of passing to the offspring. With two parents each carrying 2 desired passives and no junk passives, you have about a 10–20% chance per egg of getting all 4. That's why you hatch in bulk — breed 10–20 eggs and you'll likely get at least one winner.

❓ FAQ — 7 Common Questions

Quick answers to the questions players search for most.

Q1: How does the Palworld breeding calculator work?

Every Pal has a hidden Breeding Power value (90 for Jetragon to 1500 for Chikipi). When you breed two Pals, the game averages their Breeding Power values — and produces the Pal species whose Breeding Power is closest to that average. Our calculator does this math instantly. It's 100% deterministic for species: the same parents always produce the same child. For example, Penking (BP ~520) + Bushi (BP ~640) averages to ~580, which is closest to Anubis (BP 570). That's why Penking + Bushi = Anubis.

Q2: How to use the Palworld breeding farm?

  1. Reach Level 19 and unlock the Breeding Farm (2 Technology Points).
  2. Build it: Wood ×100, Stone ×20, Fiber ×50 within your base radius.
  3. Throw one male and one female Pal at the farm.
  4. Put Cake in the farm's chest: Flour ×5 + Red Berries ×8 + Milk ×7 + Egg ×8 + Honey ×2, cooked at a Cooking Pot.
  5. Wait ~5 minutes for the progress bar to fill. Pick up the egg.
  6. Place the egg in an Egg Incubator and wait for it to hatch.

Q4: What is the shortest breeding path in Palworld?

The shortest path depends on which Pals you already own. Use our calculator's Reverse Search to find all parent pairs for your target, then pick the pair you can most easily obtain. Well-known efficient paths:

Q6: Does Palworld breeding change with updates in 2026?

Yes. The breeding table was overhauled when Palworld moved from Early Access to 1.0, and new Pals added in updates (like Sakurajima) shift the Breeding Power list — which can change existing combinations. Our calculator is updated for Palworld 1.0 with all 262 Pals. If you're looking at a breeding chart from 2024 or early 2025, it's outdated — throw it out.

Q7: How do I breed Pals with perfect passives?

The calculator handles species, not passives. For perfect passives like Demon God + Serenity + Musclehead + Ferocious:

  1. Capture source Pals — Legendary bosses carry "Legend." Wild Pals carry Musclehead, Ferocious, Serenity. Breed for Demon God using Special Cake (Mutation system).
  2. Breed parents with 1–2 desired passives each — fewer junk passives = cleaner inheritance.
  3. Hatch in bulk — 20–100+ eggs for a 4-passive perfect Pal is normal.
  4. Use good offspring as new parents — when you get a Pal with 2–3 desired passives, swap it in.
  5. Repeat until all 4 passives land on the same Pal.
