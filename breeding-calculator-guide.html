⚡ Quick Answer: What a Breeding Calculator Does

If you only read one paragraph, read this one.

A Palworld breeding calculator tells you which two Pals to put in the Breeding Farm to get a specific offspring — or the reverse: which child you'll get from two parents you already own. Every Pal has a hidden "Breeding Power" number (typically 10–1500), and the game averages the parents' values to determine the child species. The calculator does this math for you, instantly. Pick palworld.gg if you want the fastest single-result lookup, or Palpedia if you need multi-step breeding chains and "My Pal Box" route planning. Once you have your recipe, you'll need a Breeding Farm (Level 19), Cake, and an Egg Incubator to turn that calculator result into a hatched Pal.

Why the calculator works: Breeding in Palworld is 100% deterministic for species. The same two parents always produce the same child. There is zero RNG on species — only on passives, IVs, and gender. The calculator isn't guessing; it's just doing the Breeding Power math that the game does internally.

✅ Before You Open the Calculator

Don't open a breeding calculator with no plan. Here's what you need in-game first — otherwise the calculator result is useless.

Pre-Flight Checklist

Minimum Requirements: If you haven't done these four things, close the calculator tab and go back to the game. The calculator can't help you yet.
  • ☑ Reach Level 19 — This unlocks the Breeding Farm in the Technology tree. No farm = breeding doesn't exist. Fastest way: catch 10 of each Pal species (bonus XP per first 10 catches).
  • ☑ Unlock the Breeding Farm — Costs 2 Technology Points at Level 19. Build cost: Wood ×100, Stone ×20, Fiber ×50.
  • ☑ Build an Egg Incubator — Unlocked at Level 7 (Ancient Technology). Requires: Paldium Fragments ×10, Cloth ×5, Ancient Civilization Parts ×2. Pro tip: build 3–4 incubators so you can hatch multiple eggs in parallel.
  • ☑ Automate Cake Production — Without Cake in the Breeding Farm's chest, no egg appears. You need:
    • Wheat Plantation → Mill → Flour (×5)
    • Berry Plantation → Red Berries (×8)
    • Mozzarina at Ranch → Milk (×7)
    • Chikipi at Ranch → Eggs (×8)
    • Elizabee at Ranch → Honey (×2)
    • Cooking Pot + Kindling Pal → cook into Cake

Know Your Pal Box

Before typing anything into a calculator, open your in-game Pal Box and take stock. Note down:

  • Which Pals you already own (especially rare ones with low Breeding Power)
  • Their genders — you need one male + one female of the parents the calculator suggests
  • Their passive skills — if you're breeding for passives, you need parents carrying the right ones

This 2-minute inventory saves you from the most common frustration: finding the perfect breeding recipe, then realizing you don't have a female of one parent.

🧬 How to Use the Calculator — All 3 Modes

Every breeding calculator supports three core workflows. Here's exactly how to use each one, with screenshots of what to expect.

Mode 1: Find the Offspring (Parents → Child)

Use when: You have two Pals in your box and want to know what they'll produce.

  1. Open the calculator — go to palworld.gg or Palpedia.
  2. Select Parent A — type a Pal name or scroll the dropdown. Example: "Anubis".
  3. Select Parent B — same process. Example: "Frostallion".
  4. Read the result — the calculator instantly shows the child species. That's it — one step.
Parent order does not matter. Anubis + Frostallion = Frostallion + Anubis. The game averages Breeding Power, and average is commutative. If a calculator shows different results for swapped parents, it's bugged — use a different one.

Mode 2: Reverse Search (Child → Parents)

Use when: You want a specific Pal (e.g., Jetragon) and need to know which parents can produce it.

  1. Switch to "Find Parents" mode — most calculators have a tab or toggle for this.
  2. Search your target Pal — type "Jetragon" (or whichever Pal you want).
  3. Review the list of possible pairs — the calculator shows every valid male + female combination that produces your target.
  4. Pick the easiest pair — scan the list and choose the parents you already own or can most easily catch.

For popular targets, you'll often see 5–20+ possible parent combinations. Don't just pick the first one. Scroll through and find the pair where you already have both Pals — or where both are easy to catch near your current level.

Mode 3: Shortest Path / Breeding Tree

Use when: Your target Pal requires parents you don't own — and those parents also require breeding. You need a multi-step plan.

  1. Use Palpedia for this — palworld.gg doesn't support multi-step chains.
  2. Enter your target Pal — e.g., "Shadowbeak".
  3. Click "Shortest Path" or "Breeding Tree" — Palpedia calculates the minimum number of breeding steps from wild-caught Pals to your target.
  4. Optional: Use "My Pal Box" — input the Pals you already own. Palpedia recalculates the route based on your actual inventory, potentially cutting out steps.
  5. Follow the tree step by step — work from the bottom (wild-caught) up to your target. Breed each intermediate step in order.
Example: Shadowbeak shortest path. Palpedia might show: Kitsun + Astegon → Shadowbeak. But if you don't own Astegon, it chains further: Helzephyr + Cryolinx → Astegon, then Kitsun + Astegon → Shadowbeak. That's a 2-step chain. The calculator maps all of this automatically — you just follow the arrows.

Beyond the Calculator: Breeding for Perfect Passives

The calculator only tells you the species. If you want a Pal with specific passive skills (e.g., Demon God + Serenity + Musclehead + Ferocious), you need to layer passive breeding on top:

  1. Use the calculator to get the right species first.
  2. Capture wild Pals with desired passives. Legendary bosses always carry "Legend." Musclehead and Ferocious appear on wild Pals randomly.
  3. Breed parents that each carry 1–2 desired passives — fewer unwanted passives on each parent = less dilution in the offspring.
  4. Hatch eggs in batches. Passive inheritance is random. Expect 20–100+ eggs for a perfect 4-passive Pal.
  5. When you get an offspring with 2–3 desired passives, use it as a new parent to consolidate passives into one bloodline.
  6. Repeat until all 4 passives land on the same Pal.
Passive inheritance odds (community estimate): Each passive on a parent has roughly a 40–60% chance of passing to the offspring. With two parents each carrying 2 desired passives and no junk passives, you have about a 10–20% chance per egg of getting all 4. That's why you hatch in bulk.

❓ FAQ — 8 Common Questions

The questions players actually search for — answered with specifics, not generalities.

Q1: How do breeding stats work in Palworld?

Breeding doesn't directly use visible stats (HP, ATK, DEF). Instead, every Pal has a hidden "Breeding Power" value — typically between 10 and 1500. Lower numbers = rarer Pals. When you breed two Pals, the game averages their Breeding Power values, then finds the Pal species whose Breeding Power is closest to that average. That species becomes the child.

This is why seemingly random combos produce rare Pals — it's pure math, not species logic. For example, Penking (Breeding Power ~520) + Bushi (~640) averages to ~580, which is closest to Anubis (~570). That's why Penking + Bushi = Anubis.

Q2: What is the best breeding calculator in Palworld?

palworld.gg for quick single-result lookups (A+B=?). Palpedia for multi-step breeding chains, shortest-path planning, and "My Pal Box" route optimization. See the full comparison table above to match a calculator to your specific situation.

Q3: How to use the Palworld breeding farm?

  1. Reach Level 19 and unlock the Breeding Farm from the Technology tab (costs 2 Technology Points).
  2. Build it: Wood ×100, Stone ×20, Fiber ×50. Must be placed within your base's build radius.
  3. Pick up one male and one female Pal, throw them at the farm (or assign via Monitoring Stand).
  4. Put Cake in the farm's chest on the side. Recipe: Flour ×5 + Red Berries ×8 + Milk ×7 + Egg ×8 + Honey ×2, cooked at a Cooking Pot.
  5. Wait for the progress bar to fill (~5 minutes). Pick up the egg.
  6. Place the egg in an Egg Incubator and wait for it to hatch.

Q5: What is the shortest breeding path in Palworld?

The "shortest path" depends entirely on what Pals you already own. That's why Palpedia's "My Pal Box" feature is so valuable — it calculates routes from your actual inventory. Some well-known efficient paths:

  • Anubis: Penking + Bushi (1 step, both catchable pre-Level 30)
  • Jormuntide Ignis: Blazamut + Jormuntide (1 step, both require late-game catches)
  • Shadowbeak: Kitsun + Astegon (Astegon itself requires Helzephyr + Cryolinx, making it a 2-step chain)

Use a calculator's "shortest path" feature — don't guess. Breeding chains that seem obvious often have shorter alternatives you wouldn't think of.

Q6: Where can I find a Palworld breeding chart with pictures?

Palpedia (palpedia.net/breeding) shows Pal thumbnails for every combination, which is the closest thing to a visual breeding chart. For a printable or community-made chart, check:

  • Palworld Wiki (palworld.wiki.gg) — official wiki with breeding tables
  • Reddit r/Palworld — search "breeding chart" for community-made visual guides (check dates — pre-1.0 charts are outdated)

Q7: Does Palworld breeding change with updates in 2026?

Yes. The breeding table was overhauled when Palworld moved from Early Access to 1.0. New Pals added in updates (like the Sakurajima update) shift the Breeding Power list, which can change existing combinations. Always use a calculator marked "updated for Sakurajima" or "2026 compatible." Both palworld.gg and Palpedia maintain current data. If you're looking at a breeding chart from 2024 or early 2025, it's wrong — throw it out.

Q8: How do I breed Pals with perfect passives in Palworld?

The calculator gives you the species, not the passives. For perfect passives like Demon God + Serenity + Musclehead + Ferocious:

  1. Capture source Pals — Legendary bosses carry "Legend." Wild Pals randomly carry Musclehead, Ferocious, Serenity. Breed for Demon God using Special Cake (Mutation system).
  2. Breed parents with 1–2 desired passives each — fewer junk passives on parents = higher chance of clean inheritance.
  3. Hatch in bulk — 20–100+ eggs for a 4-passive perfect Pal is normal.
  4. Use good offspring as new parents — when you get a Pal with 2–3 desired passives, swap it in to consolidate the gene pool.
  5. Repeat until all 4 passives land on the same Pal.

Community estimate: With two clean parents (each carrying 2 desired passives, no junk), you have roughly a 10–20% chance per egg of inheriting all 4. Breed 10–20 eggs and you'll likely get at least one winner.
