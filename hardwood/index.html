How to Get Hardwood in Palworld

Hardwood is a core crafting material in Palworld, needed for advanced weapons, structures, and Pal Gear from mid-game onward. The fastest way to farm it is with a Metal Axe (or better) in the Volcano biome (Mount Obsidian), where large dead trees drop 5–10 hardwood each. If you're still in the early game (level 1–15), chop small dead trees in the Grasslands with a Stone Axe — it's slow but enough to get started. If you want passive income while doing other things, build a Logging Site and assign a Pal with Lumbering Lv 2+.

📋 Quick Takeaways

  1. Volcano biome (Mount Obsidian) has the highest hardwood density — go southwest from the starting area.
  2. Metal Axe is the efficiency breakpoint — upgrading from Stone to Metal roughly 5× your hardwood per minute.
  3. Bring a carry-weight Pal (Cattiva, large mount, or anything with high capacity) to avoid multiple trips.
  4. Passive income works overnight — a Logging Site with Lumbering Lv 2+ Pal produces ~150 hardwood while you sleep.
  5. Don't farm Grasslands past early game — the small trees there have terrible drop rates. Move to Desert or Volcano biome as soon as you have a Metal Axe.
  6. One run = several hundred hardwood — a 10-minute Volcano biome trip with Refined Metal Axe yields enough for most mid-game crafting needs.
  7. Lumbering Pals at base stack — multiple Logging Sites with multiple Pals multiply your passive income.

For Manual Farming (Carry Weight)

While you chop trees, bring a Pal with high weight capacity. Cattiva (early game), Broncherry (mid game), or any large riding mount will let you carry more hardwood per trip.

Passive vs Manual: Which Should You Use?

If you're actively playing, manual farming in the Volcano biome is 10–20× faster than passive production. Use passive (Logging Site) for overnight or while you're doing other in-game activities like boss hunting or exploring. A good rhythm: do one 10-minute Volcano run when you need a burst of hardwood, then let your base Pals trickle in more between runs.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Where do I find Hardwood in Palworld?
Hardwood drops from large dead trees and fallen logs, most commonly in the Volcano biome (Mount Obsidian) and Desert biome. You need at least a Metal Axe to harvest it efficiently. Small dead trees in the starting area can also drop hardwood but at a much lower rate.
What tool do I need to get Hardwood?
You need a Metal Axe or better. A Stone Axe will get some hardwood from small dead trees but at very low efficiency (roughly 1/3 the rate). A Refined Metal Axe is the best tool for manual farming. You can also assign Pals with Lumbering suitability to a Logging Site at your base for passive hardwood income.
What is the fastest way to farm Hardwood?
The fastest method is using a Refined Metal Axe in the Volcano biome, targeting large dead trees. Bring a Pal with high carrying capacity to avoid multiple trips. For passive income, build a Logging Site and assign Pals with Lumbering Lv 2+.
Which Pals are best for farming Hardwood?
For passive farming: Bushi (Lumbering Lv 3), Wumpo Botan (Lv 3 + Transporting), Eikthyrdeer (Lv 2, easy early game). For manual hauling: any Pal with high carrying capacity — Cattiva early, Broncherry mid, large mounts late game.
Can I get Hardwood without an axe?
Yes — build a Logging Site at your base and assign Pals with Lumbering suitability. They will produce hardwood passively. The rate is slower than manual farming (~15–20 per hour per Pal), but it works while you're offline or doing other things.
Which biome has the most Hardwood?
The Volcano biome (Mount Obsidian, southwest) has the highest density of large dead trees. The Desert biome (north of starting area) is second best. Avoid Grasslands — dead trees there are mostly small and drop very little hardwood.
What is Hardwood used for in Palworld?
Hardwood is used for crafting advanced weapons (Crossbow, Metal Spear), higher-tier structures, base upgrades, and certain Pal Gear items. Expect to need several hundred hardwood for your first round of mid-game upgrades.
Is there a Pal that increases Hardwood drops while riding?
No Pal directly boosts hardwood drop rates while riding. However, Pals with the Logging Foreman partner skill boost player logging efficiency when in your party. For base production, focus on Pals with high Lumbering work suitability (Lv 3+) at your Logging Site.
