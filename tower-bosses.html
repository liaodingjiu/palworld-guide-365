⚠️ Before You Read: This Isn't the Old 5-Boss Guide

If you last played during Early Access, everything about tower bosses has changed. Palworld 1.0 added 4 new towers (9 total), slashed the fight timer from 10 minutes to 5 minutes, redesigned every arena with unique mechanics, and introduced Hard Mode for rematches.

The 5 original bosses (Zoe through Victor) still exist, but their levels, locations, and strategies have shifted. Most importantly: towers are no longer optional — 8 of them drop Key Spheres required to unlock the final World Tree boss.

🎯 New to 1.0? Jump to Saya & Selyne, Bjorn & Bastigor, Auri & Shaolong, and Zanara & Astralym for the 4 new fights.

⚔ Tower Boss System Explained

How tower bosses work in Palworld 1.0 — the 5-minute timer, Key Spheres, Hard Mode, and what's changed from Early Access.

Tower Boss Locations Map

Approximate positions on the Palpagos Islands. Numbers match the list below.

1 Zoe & Grizzbolt — Lv 10, Rayne Syndicate (112, -428)
2 Lily & Lyleen — Lv 25, Free Pal Alliance (181, 30)
3 Axel & Orserk — Lv 40, Eternal Pyre (-588, -514)
4 Marcus & Faleris — Lv 45, PIDF Tower (558, 332)
5 Victor & Shadowbeak — Lv 50, Genetic Research (-146, 450)

In Palworld 1.0, there are nine tower bosses across the Palpagos Islands, Feybreak, and Sunreach. Each boss is a syndicate leader or faction commander paired with their signature Pal. The original five towers (Zoe through Victor) return from Early Access but with redesigned arenas and updated mechanics — plus four brand-new bosses that conclude the main storyline.

Every tower boss fight now operates on a 5-minute timer (down from 10 minutes in Early Access). This is the single biggest change returning players need to adjust to — slow attrition builds no longer work. You need burst damage, proper elemental counters, and upgraded weapons for every fight. If you fail, you're ejected from the tower with no penalty beyond lost time: your Pals are not consumed, and you keep any XP earned during the attempt.

Upon first-time defeat, each of the first 8 tower bosses drops 5 Ancient Technology Points (45 total across 9 bosses) plus Ancient Civilization Parts for crafting high-tier gear. More importantly, the first 8 towers drop Key Spheres — collectibles required to unlock the final World Tree boss (Zanara & Astralym). Towers are no longer optional side content; they are the main story progression path.

Bosses do not scale to your level. Each has a fixed level and fixed HP pool, and their arenas feature unique environmental mechanics (rising water, volcanic heat, tornado barriers). You should approach them in order: Zoe (Lv 10) → Lily (Lv 20) → Axel (Lv 30) → Marcus (Lv 40) → Victor (Lv 50) → Saya (Lv 55) → Bjorn (Lv 60) → Auri (Lv 68) → Zanara (Lv 80). Some later towers have additional entry requirements — Bjorn requires 3 bounty tokens, Auri requires disabling 6 defense modules.

⚠️ Critical 1.0 Changes: (1) Timer is 5 minutes, not 10 — burst damage meta. (2) Towers 1-8 drop Key Spheres needed for final boss. (3) Tower bosses cannot be captured (EA glitch patched). (4) Hard Mode unlocks after beating Auri — rematch all bosses at Lv 72-80 for cosmetic hats. (5) Up to 4 players can enter together in multiplayer.

1 Zoe & Grizzbolt — Rayne Syndicate Tower

Level 10 · Location: Rayne Syndicate Tower · Coords: (112, -428)

Zoe is the first tower boss and fights alongside Grizzbolt, a large Electric-type Pal. Grizzbolt's main attacks include a close-range body slam, a three-bolt lightning volley that tracks your position, and a charged beam attack that deals heavy damage in a straight line. The body slam has a wind-up animation — when Grizzbolt raises its arms, immediately sprint sideways to dodge.

Since Grizzbolt is an Electric type, Ground-type Pals are your best counter. At level 10, your options are limited, but Digtoise and Fuddler are both catchable early and deal Ground-element damage. Equip your Pal with the strongest active skill available and use a mounted Pal like Direhowl to maintain mobility during the fight. Bring a bow or crossbow with at least 50 arrows.

The key strategy is to keep moving. Grizzbolt is slow, so circle around it while your Pal attacks from behind. When Grizzbolt begins charging its beam, break line of sight by hiding behind the arena's central pillar. Avoid staying directly in front of the boss at all times. With a level 10+ Pal and decent dodging, this fight is very manageable for new players.

2 Lily & Lyleen — Free Pal Alliance Tower

Level 20 · Location: Free Pal Alliance Tower · Coords: (181, 30)

Lily is the second tower boss, paired with Lyleen, a Grass-type Pal known for its elegant but deadly attacks. Lyleen's attack pattern includes a spread of homing seed projectiles, a ground-based root eruption that deals damage in a line, and a wide-area pollen burst that can stagger you if you are too close. The root eruption telegraphs with a glowing line on the ground — step off it immediately.

Lyleen is a Grass type, making Fire-type Pals the optimal counter. By level 25, you should have access to Foxparks, Arsox, or even Vanwyrm as a flying Fire-type mount. Vanwyrm is especially good here because its aerial vantage point helps you dodge the ground-based root attacks entirely. Bring Fire Arrows or a Fire-element weapon if you have one crafted.

The arena has multiple elevated platforms — use them to break line of sight from the homing seeds. When Lyleen begins its pollen burst attack (indicated by a glowing aura), back away to a safe distance before re-engaging. Keep your Fire Pal's active skills on cooldown rotation and weave in ranged weapon fire between dodges. If you have a Pal with the Flamethrower skill, it shreds Lyleen's health bar rapidly. This fight is straightforward with proper Fire-type counters.

3 Axel & Orserk — Brothers of the Eternal Pyre

Level 30 · Location: Brothers of the Eternal Pyre · Coords: (-588, -514)

Axel is the third tower boss and commands Orserk, a powerful Electric/Dragon-type Pal. Orserk's arsenal includes a devastating lightning storm that strikes multiple zones in the arena, a sweeping tail whip with wide reach, and a charged Dragon Breath that deals massive damage in a cone. The lightning storm is the most dangerous — orange markers appear on the ground before each strike, giving you about two seconds to reposition.

Orserk is Electric/Dragon, so Ice and Ground types are your best options. Frostallion is ideal if you can acquire it, but at level 40, Reindrix, Cryolinx, or Penking are solid Ice-type alternatives. For Ground coverage, Anubis or Digtoise work well. A dual-type approach — one Ice Pal for damage and one Ground Pal for tanking — gives you flexibility during the fight.

The Dragon Breath attack is the fight's biggest threat. When Orserk rears back and its mouth glows, immediately dash perpendicular to the cone direction. Never stand directly in front of Orserk for more than two seconds. Use a fast mount like Direhowl or Nitewing for quick repositioning. Keep your Pals rotated — swap to a fresh Pal when your active one drops below 30% HP. With proper Ice/Ground coverage and consistent dodging, Axel falls within the timer comfortably.

4 Marcus & Faleris — PIDF Tower

Level 40 · Location: PIDF Tower · Coords: (558, 332)

Marcus is the fourth tower boss, partnered with Faleris, a swift Fire-type Pal. Faleris is the fastest tower boss in the game — it frequently dashes across the arena, leaving fire trails that deal residual damage if you walk through them. Its signature attacks include a diving fireball barrage, a rapid peck combo at close range, and a massive fire tornado that expands outward from its position. The fire tornado is the fight's signature mechanic.

Faleris is a Fire type, so Water and Ice types are your strongest counters. Jormuntide (Watering Lv4) is an excellent choice with high HP and strong Water skills. Azumurai and Penking also work well if you need accessible options. For Ice coverage, Reindrix or Cryolinx can deal super-effective damage while tanking fire hits reasonably well.

The fire tornado is your biggest threat — when Faleris begins spinning, sprint to the arena's edge and circle around. The tornado expands for about five seconds before dissipating. Avoid the fire trails on the ground, as they persist for several seconds and chip away your health. Use a flying mount to gain verticality and dodge ground-based attacks. Bring at least 80 rounds of ammunition for your best ranged weapon. With Water/Ice coverage and disciplined positioning, Marcus is beatable.

Weapon Tip: The Rocket Launcher or Grenade Launcher is extremely effective against Faleris due to its large hitbox. Craft at least 10 rockets before attempting this fight — each hit deals massive burst damage that bypasses Faleris's speed advantage.

5 Victor & Shadowbeak — PAL Genetic Research Tower

Level 50 · Location: PAL Genetic Research Tower · Coords: (-146, 450)

Victor is the final tower boss, commanding Shadowbeak, a Dark-type Pal with some of the highest DPS in the game. Shadowbeak's attack pattern is aggressive and fast: it fires a volley of dark energy projectiles that home in on your position, executes a rapid dive-bomb that deals massive contact damage, and unleashes Divine Disaster — its signature skill that creates an expanding shockwave dealing catastrophic area damage. Divine Disaster has a very short wind-up, making it hard to react to.

Shadowbeak is a Dark type, which means Dragon-type Pals are your best counter. Jetragon is the top pick — its Dragon Meteor skill deals enormous burst damage, and its flight speed lets you dodge Shadowbeak's dive-bombs. If you don't have Jetragon yet, Jormuntide Ignis or Relaxaurus can serve as Dragon-type alternatives with solid damage output.

This fight is a DPS race against the 10-minute timer. Start by deploying Jetragon and immediately using Dragon Meteor. When Shadowbeak charges Divine Disaster (look for the dark aura pulse), sprint directly away — the shockwave has limited range and can be outrun with a fast mount. Use the arena's central structure to break homing projectile locks. Keep a second Pal ready as backup. If your first Pal faints, swap immediately rather than wasting time reviving. Bring your best weapon — the Legendary Assault Rifle or Rocket Launcher — and unload between dodges. Victor is the hardest fight in the game, but with Jetragon and proper execution, it's very winnable.

Final Boss Tip: If you're struggling with the 10-minute timer, breed a Jetragon with passive skills like Musclehead, Ferocious, and Lucky. These passives stack to increase attack damage by over 50%, dramatically shortening the fight. A fully optimized Jetragon can defeat Shadowbeak in under 5 minutes.

6 Saya & Selyne — Moonflower Tower NEW

Level 55 · Location: Moonflower Tower, Sakurajima · Coords: (-602, 214)

Saya is the sixth tower boss, introduced in the Sakurajima update and carried into 1.0. She commands Selyne, a sleek Dark-type Pal that's faster and more evasive than Victor's Shadowbeak. Selyne's attack pattern includes rapid shadow dashes that leave damaging afterimages, a wide-angle dark beam sweep, and a charged Lunar Barrage that rains projectiles across the entire arena. Selyne is harder to pin down than Shadowbeak, but has a slightly smaller HP pool.

Selyne is a Dark type, so Dragon-type Pals are your optimal counter — the same team you used for Victor carries straight over. Jetragon with Dragon Meteor remains the top pick. Astegon, Jormuntide, and Blazamut Ryu are solid alternatives. If you just beat Victor, you can challenge Saya immediately with the same party setup — the two Dark-type towers are designed to be cleared back-to-back.

The key to this fight is patience. Selyne's dashes have brief recovery windows — hold your heaviest skills for when it commits to an attack, then unload during the recovery animation. The Lunar Barrage is telegraphed by Selyne floating upward with a glowing aura; sprint to the arena edge and weave between impact zones. Don't chase Selyne around the arena — let it come to you. A Legendary Assault Rifle with 150+ rounds is the recommended weapon tier for this fight.

🎯 Efficiency Tip: Victor (Lv 50) and Saya (Lv 55) are both Dark-type bosses weak to Dragon. Clear Victor first, then head straight to Sakurajima with the same Jetragon-led team. Two Ancient Tech Points in one play session with zero party changes.

7 Bjorn & Bastigor — Feybreak Tower NEW

Level 60 · Location: Feybreak Tower, Feybreak Island · Coords: (-1,288, -1,665)

Bjorn is the seventh tower boss, guarding the Feybreak region's ancient tower. He fights alongside Bastigor, a massive Ice-type Pal with devastating frost attacks. Bastigor's moveset includes a charging ice lance that tracks your position, a ground-freezing AoE that slows movement, and a Glacial Collapse ultimate that deals massive damage in a wide radius. The difficulty spikes noticeably here — this is where many players hit their first wall.

⚠️ Entry Requirement: You cannot simply walk into the Feybreak Tower. You must first defeat three Alpha Pal bounties on Feybreak Island to obtain entry tokens:

  • Dazzi Noct (Bounty Token) — Coords: -1,343, -1,579
  • Caprity Noct (Bounty Token) — Coords: -1,169, -1,654
  • Omascul (Bounty Token) — Coords: -1,183, -1,496

Collect all three tokens before approaching the tower at (-1,294, -1,669) or you'll be turned away at the door.

Bastigor is Ice type, weak to Fire. Knocklem Ignis is the standout counter — its Fire/Ground dual typing resists ice while dealing super-effective damage. Foxparks (with its flamethrower Partner Skill), Ragnahawk, and Vanwyrm are also strong picks. Field a full team of Fire Pals — don't mix types here, Bastigor's ice damage will shred anything not resistant. Bring Heat Resistant armor (the Feybreak tower interior is surprisingly cold — the opposite of what you'd expect, so Cold Resistant armor is actually what you need for the region).

Recommended level is 53+ with at least a Heavy Assault Rifle and 200+ rounds. Beating Bjorn unlocks the path to Sunreach and the final stretch of the game.

🗺️ Navigation Note: Feybreak Island is in the far southwest corner of the map. Bring a fast flying mount (Jetragon or Frostallion) — the journey from central Palpagos takes several minutes even at max flight speed. Set up a temporary base or fast travel point nearby before attempting the tower.

8 Auri & Shaolong — Tower of the Azure Covenant NEW

Level 68 · Location: Tower of the Azure Covenant, Sunreach · Coords: (-433, -1,424)

Auri is the eighth tower boss, found in the floating sky islands of Sunreach. She commands Shaolong, a majestic Water/Dragon-type Pal with some of the most complex arena mechanics in the game. This fight has multiple phases and is widely considered the most mechanically demanding tower boss — even more so than the final boss for some players.

⚠️ Entry Requirement: You must first defeat Bjorn at Feybreak, then fly to Sunreach and disable all six defense modules scattered across the floating islands. These modules generate a tornado barrier around the tower — approach before disabling them and you'll be thrown back. The modules are marked on your map once you enter Sunreach airspace.

Shaolong is Water/Dragon dual-type, giving it two weaknesses: Electric and Ice. Orserk (Electric/Dragon) is the ideal counter — it resists Dragon attacks while exploiting the Electric weakness. Frostallion (Ice) is the best pure-Ice option. Grizzbolt and Vanwyrm Cryst are solid budget alternatives. Bring at least 2-3 dedicated counter Pals — this is not a fight you can brute-force with neutral damage.

Critical mechanic: Aim for Shaolong's blue body — the white fur doesn't register damage (similar to Grizzbolt headshot mechanics). Mid-fight, the arena floor floods with water, leaving only stone pillars to stand on — bring a flying or swimming mount or you'll drown. Shaolong also periodically drags your active Pal to the arena center and unleashes a punishing AoE — recall your Pal immediately when you see the pull animation, or redeploy if it's already being hit. Legendary-tier weapons (Plasma Rifle or Beam Launcher) matter more than perfect Pal IVs in this fight.

🏆 Milestone: Defeating Auri unlocks Hard Mode for all tower bosses, allowing you to rematch every boss at Level 72-80 for exclusive cosmetic hats. It also opens the final questline — "The Sealed Calamity" — leading to the World Tree and the true final boss.

9 Zanara & Astralym — The Sealed Sanctum FINAL

Level 80 · Location: World Tree, The Sealed Sanctum · HP: ~420,000

Zanara is the true final boss of Palworld 1.0, fought at the World Tree's Sealed Sanctum. She commands Astralym, a cosmic entity that is completely typeless — it has no elemental weaknesses whatsoever. This is the ultimate preparation check: if you haven't been leveling your Pals, upgrading gear, and optimizing your team, this fight will expose every gap in your build.

⚠️ Entry Requirement: You must collect all 8 Key Spheres from the previous tower bosses plus complete the "Sealed Calamity" questline. Without all 8 spheres, the sanctum door remains locked. If you skipped any tower, go back and clear it first.

Astralym is typeless (no weaknesses), which means type advantage strategies don't apply. Bring your absolute highest-damage Pals regardless of element. The meta picks for this fight are: Jetragon (flying mobility + missile launcher), Xenolord, Frostallion, Necromus (melee DPS), Bastigor (tank), and Lovander (Life Steal sustain). A fully optimized team of Level 80 Pals with maxed passive skills is the expected standard.

Gear requirements: Enter at Level 80 with full Ancient-tier armor (Helm, Armor, Shield), a Plasma Rifle or Beam Launcher with 300+ rounds, and maxed Statue of Power buffs. Astralym's attacks have very short telegraphs and can kill you in 2-3 hits even with endgame armor — respect every wind-up animation and only commit damage during confirmed recovery windows. Its signature attack, Cosmic Rupture, creates an expanding ring of energy that covers the entire arena; sprint to the outermost edge and jump over the wave. Recommended team composition: Jetragon (flight/missiles), Necromus (raw DPS), Orserk (strong attacks), Bastigor (damage sponge), and Lovander (healing sustain).

This fight is doable solo but significantly easier in multiplayer (up to 4 players). Coordinate Pal deployments so someone always has aggro while others unload from range.

🏁 Final Prep Checklist: ☐ Level 80 ☐ All 8 Key Spheres ☐ Ancient Armor/Shield/Helm ☐ Plasma Rifle or Beam Launcher + 300 ammo ☐ Max Statue of Power buffs ☐ 5 Lv 80 Pals with optimized passives (Musclehead, Ferocious, Lucky, Serenity) ☐ Flying mount for mobility ☐ Backup ammo and healing items. Skip any of these and you're in for a rough time.

🔥 Hard Mode & Endgame Tower Rematches

How to unlock Hard Mode, what changes, and whether the cosmetic rewards are worth the grind.

Hard Mode unlocks after defeating Auri & Shaolong (Tower 8 in Sunreach). Once unlocked, you can revisit any of the first 8 towers and challenge them at dramatically increased difficulty. Hard Mode bosses scale to Level 72-80 with massively inflated HP pools ranging from 464,000 to 570,000 — roughly 4-5x their normal mode values.

The primary reward for Hard Mode clears is exclusive cosmetic hats — one unique hat design per boss. These are purely cosmetic (no stat bonuses) but serve as prestige items showing you've mastered every tower. Hard Mode bosses also drop significantly more Ancient Civilization Parts per clear and grant substantial XP, making them viable for endgame grinding.

Strategy for Hard Mode: The 5-minute timer remains, but HP pools are massive — you need perfectly optimized DPS to clear within the time limit. This means Lv 80 Pals with stacked damage passives (Musclehead + Ferocious + Lucky + Serenity), Legendary-tier weapons, and zero wasted time on dodging. Learn every boss's attack pattern in Normal Mode first — in Hard Mode, one missed dodge can cost you the timer. The cosmetic hats are account-wide unlocks, so you only need to clear each Hard Mode boss once.

💡 Hard Mode Priority Order: Start with Zoe (easiest Hard Mode, good for practice) → Lily → Axel → Marcus → Victor → Saya → Bjorn → Auri (hardest). Don't attempt Hard Mode until you're Level 80 with fully optimized endgame gear — the HP scaling is no joke.

💡 General Boss Fight Tips (1.0 Updated)

Universal strategies that apply to every tower boss fight in Palworld 1.0 — including the tighter 5-minute timer.

⏱️ The 5-Minute Timer Changes Everything

The timer reduction from 10 minutes to 5 minutes is the single biggest adjustment returning players need to make. This means: (1) slow, defensive strategies no longer work — you must play aggressively; (2) elemental counters are no longer optional, they're mandatory for meeting the DPS check; (3) weapon upgrades matter more than ever — a tier-behind weapon can cost you the clear. If you're consistently timing out, upgrade your weapon one tier and try again. See our Best Weapons guide for optimal loadouts at each level.

🔄 Pal Recall Strategy (1.0 Meta)

Recall your Pal before heavy telegraphed attacks — this is the most important skill in 1.0 boss fights. When a boss winds up a big AoE (glowing aura, charging animation), immediately recall your active Pal. A recalled Pal takes zero damage and can be redeployed instantly. If the Pal gets hit and faints, you lose 10-15 seconds of DPS waiting for the redeploy cooldown — that's 3-5% of your total timer. Redeploying a healthy Pal is always faster than reviving a fainted one. Bind your Pal recall key to something you can press instantly.

Dodge Timing

Every boss attack has a wind-up animation. Learn to read these cues: raised arms indicate a slam, glowing mouths signal beam attacks, and ground markers warn of area effects. The dodge window is typically 1-2 seconds after the wind-up begins. Sprint perpendicular to the attack direction — never back away in a straight line, as tracking projectiles will still hit you. Use arena pillars to break line of sight on homing attacks.

Mount Usage

Flying mounts like Vanwyrm, Nitewing, and Jetragon give you aerial superiority against ground-based attacks. When mounted, your Pal's partner skill often becomes available — Jetragon's missile launcher is a free DPS cooldown. Always enter a boss fight mounted, and remount immediately if you get knocked off. For Auri's tower specifically, a flying mount is mandatory due to the flooded arena phase. See our Best Mounts guide for speed and combat rankings.

Weapon Progression by Tower

Your personal weapon damage is critical to beating the 5-minute timer. Recommended weapon tier per boss:

  • Towers 1-2 (Zoe, Lily): Bow/Crossbow → Metal Spear or Musket
  • Towers 3-4 (Axel, Marcus): Musket → Single-Shot Rifle or Heavy Assault Rifle
  • Towers 5-6 (Victor, Saya): Legendary Assault Rifle (200+ rounds) or Rocket Launcher
  • Towers 7-8 (Bjorn, Auri): Plasma Rifle or Beam Launcher (250+ rounds)
  • Tower 9 (Zanara): Max-tier Plasma Rifle or Beam Launcher (300+ rounds) + Rocket Launcher backup

Armor & Environment Requirements

Several towers require specific armor to avoid passive damage before and during the fight. Axel's tower (Mount Obsidian volcano): Heat-resistant armor mandatory. Victor's tower (Astral Mountains): Cold-resistant armor mandatory. Marcus's tower (Desert): Daytime heat + nighttime cold — bring both or time your attempt for morning. Bjorn's tower (Feybreak): Cold-resistant armor. Don't forget to repair your armor before each attempt — entering with damaged gear is a common mistake.

Pal Party Composition

Bring a party of 5 Pals to every boss fight. Include at least two type-advantage Pals for damage, one tanky Pal to absorb aggro, and rotate your active Pal when it drops below 30% HP. A fainted Pal deals zero DPS and the redeploy timer costs precious seconds. For Hard Mode and the final boss, breed your Pals with stacked damage passives: Musclehead + Ferocious + Lucky + Serenity is the meta combo. Check our Breeding Guide for how to breed perfect combat Pals.

Multiplayer Strategy

Up to 4 players can enter towers together in multiplayer. Coordinate Pal deployments so one player tanks aggro while others DPS from range. Divide elemental coverage across the party — one player brings Fire, another Ice, etc. Boss HP does NOT scale with player count, making multiplayer significantly easier than solo. If you're stuck on a boss, try bringing a friend before grinding more levels.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions — Tower Bosses

How many tower bosses are there in Palworld 1.0?
Palworld 1.0 has 9 tower bosses total — up from 5 in Early Access. The original five (Zoe, Lily, Axel, Marcus, Victor) return with updated mechanics, plus four new bosses: Saya & Selyne (Lv 55, Sakurajima), Bjorn & Bastigor (Lv 60, Feybreak), Auri & Shaolong (Lv 68, Sunreach), and Zanara & Astralym (Lv 80, World Tree — final boss).
What order should I fight the tower bosses in Palworld?
Recommended order: 1. Zoe & Grizzbolt (Lv 10) → 2. Lily & Lyleen (Lv 20) → 3. Axel & Orserk (Lv 30) → 4. Marcus & Faleris (Lv 40) → 5. Victor & Shadowbeak (Lv 50) → 6. Saya & Selyne (Lv 55) → 7. Bjorn & Bastigor (Lv 60) → 8. Auri & Shaolong (Lv 68) → 9. Zanara & Astralym (Lv 80). Towers 7 and 8 have entry requirements (bounty tokens and defense modules).
What changed about tower bosses in Palworld 1.0?
Everything. Major 1.0 changes: (1) 9 bosses total (up from 5 in EA). (2) Timer reduced from 10 minutes to 5 minutes — burst damage meta. (3) Every arena redesigned with unique mechanics. (4) New Hard Mode unlocked after beating Auri — rematch all bosses at Lv 72-80 for cosmetic hats. (5) Towers 1-8 drop Key Spheres needed for the final boss. (6) Bosses cannot be captured (EA glitch patched). (7) Up to 4 players can enter together in multiplayer.
What are the tower boss weaknesses?
Zoe & Grizzbolt (Electric) → Ground. Lily & Lyleen (Grass) → Fire. Axel & Orserk (Dragon/Electric) → Ice, Ground. Marcus & Faleris (Fire) → Water. Victor & Shadowbeak (Dark) → Dragon. Saya & Selyne (Dark) → Dragon. Bjorn & Bastigor (Ice) → Fire. Auri & Shaolong (Water/Dragon) → Electric, Ice. Zanara & Astralym — typeless, no weaknesses.
What are the best Pals for tower boss fights?
Early: Gumoss or Fuddler (Ground) vs Grizzbolt; Foxparks or Vanwyrm (Fire) vs Lyleen. Mid: Reptyro Cryst (Ice/Ground, covers both weaknesses) vs Orserk; Jormuntide (Water) vs Faleris. Late: Jetragon (Dragon) vs Shadowbeak and Selyne; Knocklem Ignis (Fire) vs Bastigor; Orserk or Frostallion vs Shaolong. Final: Jetragon, Xenolord, Necromus, Bastigor, Lovander vs Zanara.
What rewards do you get from tower bosses?
Each first clear: 5 Ancient Technology Points (45 total across 9 bosses), Ancient Civilization Parts, and a Key Sphere (towers 1-8, needed for the final boss). First clears also grant massive player XP. Bosses can be refought for additional Ancient Civilization Parts and XP. Hard Mode rewards exclusive cosmetic hats and bonus Ancient Civilization Parts.
How do you unlock Hard Mode for tower bosses?
Hard Mode unlocks after defeating Auri & Shaolong (Tower 8 in Sunreach). Once unlocked, all 8 earlier bosses can be rematched at Level 72-80 with massively increased HP (464,000 to 570,000). Each Hard Mode clear rewards an exclusive cosmetic hat. You must be Level 80 with fully optimized gear and max-level Pals to clear within the 5-minute timer.
Can you capture tower bosses in Palworld?
No. Tower boss Pals cannot be captured — the glitch that allowed capturing bosses in Early Access was patched long ago. The only exception is Zoe (the first boss), who can be recruited as an NPC through a side quest after you defeat her tower. Speak to her outside the Rayne Syndicate Tower after clearing it to start her recruitment mission. All other tower bosses are fight-only encounters — you can refight them for XP and parts, but you cannot add them to your Pal collection.
