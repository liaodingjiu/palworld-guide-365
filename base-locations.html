🏠 Understanding the Base System

How bases work in Palworld, how many you can have, and why your location choice matters more than you think.

In Palworld, your base is the heart of your operation — it's where you craft, breed, farm, smelt, and store everything you collect across the Palpagos Islands. The base system is deceptively simple but has a few critical mechanics every player should understand before committing to a location.

You can have up to 3 bases simultaneously. You start with 1 base slot at the beginning of the game. A second base slot unlocks when your first base reaches Base Level 10, and the third slot opens at Base Level 15. Each base operates independently with its own Palbox, Pals, and production lines — but your Pal capacity is shared across all bases combined (the default is 15 Pals per base, for 45 total at max).

This 3-base limit means you need to be strategic. You cannot put a base everywhere, so each location should serve a clear purpose: one for crafting and breeding, one for mining ore, and one for rare materials like quartz or sulfur. Spreading your bases across different biomes also ensures a steady supply of diverse resources without constant travel.

Location choice affects everything: how easily you can defend against raids, how fast your Pals can path between work stations, whether you have flat ground for large builds, and proximity to fast travel statues. A poor base location can bottleneck your entire progression — while a great one can automate your resource pipeline and let you focus on exploration and combat.

Key Strategy: Never build all 3 bases in the same biome. Spread them across different regions to cover ore, quartz, and crafting needs simultaneously. One specialized mining base can supply your main crafting base with materials through the storage box network.

📍 Top 6 Base Locations Analyzed

Detailed breakdown of each location — why it's great, what resources you'll find, pros and cons, and which game stage it fits best.

1. Ore Mountain — (191, -36) S Tier

Ore Mountain is widely considered the single best mining base in Palworld. Located in the central region of the map, this area features a remarkable cluster of 8+ ore nodes tightly packed around a flat plateau — meaning your mining Pals can harvest continuously without long travel paths. Stone and occasional coal deposits round out the resource mix. The terrain is naturally defensible with elevated ground on one side, making raid defense manageable with a simple wall perimeter. A fast travel statue is located nearby, giving you instant access to the rest of the map. The only real downside is that Syndicate enemies and aggressive wild Pals spawn frequently in the surrounding area, so you'll want defensive Pals stationed here. This location is ideal for mid-game players who have unlocked their second base slot and need a dedicated ore pipeline to fuel Ingots, Nails, and Cement production.

2. Ice Wind Island — (-79, -318) A+ Tier

Ice Wind Island sits in the far north and is the premier location for Pure Quartz farming in all of Palworld. Quartz nodes are scattered generously across the island's frozen landscape, making it essential for late-game players who need Circuit Boards, Polymer, and other advanced tech materials. Coal deposits are also present in smaller quantities. The island's isolation is both its greatest strength and weakness — there are very few enemy spawns here, so raids are rare and manageable, but you'll need Cold Resistant armor (or a Frostallion in your team) to survive the journey. Flat building space is limited compared to other locations, so plan your layout carefully before placing the Palbox. This spot becomes invaluable once you hit the technology tree that demands quartz in bulk. It's best suited as your third base, unlocked at Base Level 15, when you're already well-established and need to push into end-game crafting.

3. Verdant Brook — (-257, 201) A+ Tier

Verdant Brook is the best all-rounder starter base location in the game. Situated in a temperate zone with no extreme weather conditions, it offers a generous spread of ore nodes, abundant trees for wood, stone deposits, and Paldium fragment spawns — everything a new player needs within a short walking distance. The ground is large and flat, giving you plenty of room to expand your base as you unlock new structures. A river runs nearby, which is convenient for Watering-type Pals and crop irrigation. Enemy spawns here are low-level and non-aggressive, making it safe even for players who just finished the tutorial. The location is also relatively central, with fast travel statues to the north and south. Its only weakness is a lack of late-game rare resources like quartz and sulfur — but as a main crafting, breeding, and farming hub for your first 30 hours, Verdant Brook is hard to beat. Highly recommended for your very first Palbox placement.

4. Dessicated Desert — (357, -58) A Tier

The Dessicated Desert in the northeast is your go-to base for coal and sulfur production. Coal nodes are dense and numerous here, while sulfur deposits — critical for gunpowder and ammunition crafting — are found in scattered clusters across the sandy expanse. Ore is also present, making this a well-rounded mineral base. The terrain is mostly flat, which is excellent for large-scale builds with production lines and conveyor-like Pal workflows. However, the desert biome is hot, so you'll need Heat Resistant armor to avoid taking damage while building and managing your base. Aggressive desert Pals like Anubis and Felbat spawn here, so defensive walls and combat-capable Pals are a must. This location shines in the mid-to-late game when ammunition demand spikes for tower boss attempts and legendary Pal encounters. Pair it with a sulfur processing line and you'll never run out of gunpowder again.

5. Mount Obsidian — (87, -475) A Tier

Mount Obsidian is the ultimate late-game mineral hub, located in the far west volcanic region. This area is packed with coal, ore, and sulfur nodes — arguably the highest density of mixed mineral resources on the entire map. If you need to mass-produce Cement, Gunpowder, and refined metal in huge quantities, this is where you set up shop. The volcanic terrain is rugged and uneven, so finding a flat build pad requires some scouting before committing to a Palbox placement. The surrounding area is patrolled by high-level aggressive Pals, including the occasional Jetragon spawn nearby, making this completely unsuitable for early-game players. You'll want Heat Resistant armor and a team of combat-ready Pals before establishing a base here. For experienced players in the endgame phase, Mount Obsidian serves as a powerhouse production base that can feed your entire operation with raw materials for advanced crafting and breeding infrastructure.

6. Forgotten Island — (85, -220) B+ Tier

Forgotten Island is a small offshore island that offers a quiet, peaceful base experience with minimal threat from raids and enemy spawns. Resources here are modest — primarily stone, Paldium fragments, and wood — with a few ore nodes but no coal, quartz, or sulfur to speak of. The island's small size means building space is tight, so this isn't the place for sprawling production lines. However, its real value lies in safety and simplicity. New players who want a low-stress first base, or experienced players looking for a dedicated breeding facility away from danger, will find Forgotten Island ideal. The lack of aggressive neighbors means your Pals can work undisturbed, and the island's coastal position offers easy fast travel boat access. It's not a production powerhouse, but as a stable, safe outpost for egg incubation, breeding farms, or a starter learning base, it earns its place on this list.

🎯 3-Base Strategy & Recommended Setup

How to optimally distribute your 3 bases for maximum resource coverage and efficiency.

With only 3 base slots available, every choice matters. The optimal strategy is to assign each base a clear specialization so that together they cover your entire resource pipeline — from raw materials to finished products. Here's the recommended 3-base configuration that works for the vast majority of playthroughs:

Base 1: Main Hub — Verdant Brook (-257, 201)

Role: Crafting, breeding, farming, and storage HQ.
Pal Assignments: Anubis (Handiwork/Mining), Jormuntide Ignis (Kindling), Jormuntide (Watering), Lyleen (Planting/Medicine), Wumpo Botan (Transporting). See our Best Pals guide for full stats on these workers. Breeding perfect specimens with passive skills like Work Slave and Artisan dramatically boosts base efficiency.
Structures: Breeding Farm, Berry Plantation, production line (Furnace, Crusher, Workbench), feed boxes, beds.

Base 2: Ore Mine — Ore Mountain (191, -36)

Role: Dedicated ore and ingot production.
Pal Assignments: Astegon (Mining Lv4), Digtoise (Mining), Anubis (Mining/Transport), Rephgro (Transport), combat Pals for raid defense.
Structures: Multiple Furnaces, ore storage, defensive walls, watchtowers. Keep this base lean — it's a factory, not a home.

Base 3: Quartz/Sulfur — Ice Wind Island (-79, -318)

Role: Late-game rare material farming.
Pal Assignments: Astegon or Digtoise (Mining), Frostallion (Cooling for any refrigeration needs), transport Pals.
Structures: Quartz processing, storage boxes, generator. Unlocked last when you hit the tech tree that demands Pure Quartz and Circuit Boards.

Alternative Setup: If you're focused on combat and ammunition rather than tech, swap Base 3 for Dessicated Desert (357, -58) to mass-produce coal and sulfur for gunpowder. This gives you a self-sufficient ammo pipeline.
Pal Routing: Assign Pals based on their work suitability levels. A Pal with Mining Lv4 (like Astegon) belongs at your mining base, not your crafting base. Don't waste a base slot's capacity on Pals that have nothing to do there — every Pal slot is precious.

💡 Base Building Tips

Practical advice to make your bases more efficient, safe, and productive.

Build near Fast Travel statues: Before placing your Palbox, check the map for the nearest Great Eagle statue. Bases within walking distance of a fast travel point save enormous amounts of time when shuttling materials, Pals, and supplies between your three bases. You can even use fast travel to instantly transport items in your inventory — no mount required.
Flatten terrain before building: Use the Foundation building piece to create a flat platform before placing walls and workstations. Pals path poorly on uneven ground — they can get stuck on slopes, fall off cliffs, or take long detours. A flat, compact base layout dramatically improves Pal work efficiency and reduces the chance of Pals getting stuck or abandoning tasks.
Always build defensive walls: Even in "safe" locations, raids can and will happen. A simple stone wall perimeter with a single gated entrance funnels enemies into a chokepoint where your combat Pals can deal with them. Place a mounted turret or two near the entrance for extra insurance. Never leave your base completely undefended — one bad raid can destroy hours of progress.
