🔥 How Weapons Work in Palworld

Understanding weapon types, rarity tiers, workbenches, and ammo systems before you start crafting.

Weapons in Palworld are your primary tool for combat, hunting, and defense. Unlike Pals which fight autonomously, weapons are wielded directly by the player and require ammo (for ranged weapons) or stamina (for melee). The right weapon at the right time can make the difference between defeating a tower boss in 2 minutes or 20.

Weapon categories in Palworld:

  • Bows & Crossbows — Early to mid-game ranged weapons. Cheap ammo (arrows), no gunpowder needed. The Crossbow is the best early-game weapon by far.
  • Handguns & Revolvers — Mid-game firearms. Moderate damage, moderate fire rate, cheap ammo. Good for catching Pals without killing them.
  • Rifles — Mid to late-game sustained DPS weapons. The Assault Rifle is the workhorse of the late game.
  • Shotguns — Close-range burst damage. Excellent for alpha Pal encounters where you need to burst down a target quickly.
  • Launchers — Explosive weapons with massive single-hit damage but expensive ammo and slow reloads. Best for boss openers.
  • Melee Weapons — Swords, spears, and clubs. No ammo needed but requires getting close. The Sword is the only viable endgame melee weapon.
  • Throwables — Grenades with status effects. Stackable, consumable, and excellent for boss fight openers.

Rarity tiers explained:

Every craftable weapon in Palworld has 5 rarity tiers: Common → Uncommon → Rare → Epic → Legendary. Higher tiers increase the weapon's attack value significantly — for example, a Common Assault Rifle has 320 ATK while a Legendary Assault Rifle has 560 ATK, a 75% increase. To craft higher tiers, you need the corresponding Schematic in your inventory (see our schematics section below).

Workbench requirements:

  • Primitive Workbench (Lv 2) — Bows, arrows, stone tools, wooden clubs
  • Weapon Workbench (Lv 20) — Crossbows, handguns, rifles, shotguns, and their ammo (up to mid-tier)
  • Weapon Assembly Line (Lv 40) — High-tier firearms including Assault Rifle, Rocket Launcher, and endgame weapons
  • Weapon Assembly Line II (Lv 55) — Top-tier weapons including Plasma Cannon, Charge Rifle, and the latest Sakurajima update gear
Pro Tip: Don't waste Technology Points on every weapon tier. Skip interim weapons and save points for the "best in tier" options. The optimal path is: Old Bow (Lv 3) → Crossbow (Lv 13) → Musket or Makeshift Handgun (Lv 21-24) → Single-Shot Rifle (Lv 36) → Assault Rifle (Lv 45) → Rocket Launcher (Lv 49). This gives you 6 weapons that cover the entire game efficiently.

🎯 Best Ranged Weapons (Guns & Bows)

Detailed breakdown of the top ranged weapons, why they excel, and when to craft them.

1. Assault Rifle — The Best All-Around Weapon

The Assault Rifle is the backbone of any late-game loadout. Unlocking at Level 45, it fires Rifle Ammo at a high rate from a 30-round magazine, dealing 560 ATK per shot at Legendary tier. With a DPS of approximately 254 (including reload time), it offers the best sustained damage of any non-explosive weapon in the game. Rifle Ammo is also relatively cheap — 2 Gunpowder and 1 Refined Ingot per round — making the Assault Rifle affordable for extended combat sessions.

The Assault Rifle truly shines in tower boss fights and alpha Pal encounters, where its sustained DPS whittles down high-HP targets faster than any single-shot weapon. Pair it with a combat Pal like Anubis or Faleris for damage buffs, and you have a loadout that carries you through the entire endgame.

Crafting cost: 30 Carbon Fiber, 10 Polymer, 40 Refined Ingot at a Weapon Assembly Line.

2. Musket — The Sniper's Choice

The Musket is the highest-damage non-explosive ranged weapon in the game, with a staggering 2,000 ATK at Legendary tier. Unlocked at Level 21, it's one of the earliest firearms available and remains relevant through the mid-game thanks to its enormous per-shot damage. Each shot uses Coarse Ammo (1 Gunpowder + 1 Ingot), which is the cheapest firearm ammo in the game.

The tradeoff? The Musket has a slow fire rate (0.1 shots per second) and a 6-second reload time, giving it a DPS of only 330 despite its massive per-shot damage. This makes it ideal for opening shots on alpha Pals and tower bosses — fire once for huge burst, then switch to your Assault Rifle or Crossbow for sustained damage. The Musket is also excellent for catching Pals, as a single shot can chunk a wild Pal's HP without risking overkill.

3. Crossbow — The Early-Game King

The Crossbow is the best weapon for the first 15-20 hours of Palworld. Unlocking at Level 13, it deals 280 ATK per shot (490 at Legendary) and uses cheap arrows as ammo — no gunpowder required. The Crossbow outclasses every bow in the game and remains viable well into the mid-game. If you're following our beginner guide, the Crossbow should be your first major weapon craft.

The Crossbow's only weakness is its slow reload (2 seconds) and single-shot magazine. But at 233 DPS (Legendary), it actually out-damages many early firearms. Keep using the Crossbow until you can craft the Musket (Level 21) or Makeshift Handgun (Level 24).

Variants: Fire Arrow Crossbow (Lv 15) and Poison Arrow Crossbow (Lv 17) offer the same base damage but apply burn and poison status effects respectively. These are excellent for boss fights where damage-over-time compounds your DPS.

4. Single-Shot Rifle — The Mid-Game Powerhouse

Unlocking at Level 36, the Single-Shot Rifle fills the gap between the Musket and the Assault Rifle. With 2,310 ATK at Legendary and a faster fire rate than the Musket, it offers a DPS of 974 — nearly triple the Musket's output. It uses Rifle Ammo, making it ammo-efficient since Rifle Ammo is shared with the Assault Rifle and Semi-Auto Rifle.

The Single-Shot Rifle is the best weapon for the third and fourth tower bosses, offering precision damage without the ammo cost of a full-auto weapon. Once you unlock the Assault Rifle at Level 45, the Single-Shot Rifle becomes your secondary weapon for long-range engagements.

5. Pump-Action Shotgun — Close-Range Burst

The Pump-Action Shotgun (Level 43) is the best close-range weapon in the game. With 385 ATK per shot at Legendary and a 12-round magazine, it can shred alpha Pals at point-blank range. Each shell deals damage in a spread pattern, meaning you can hit multiple enemies at once — useful when fighting groups of hostile Pals.

Shotgun shells are more expensive than rifle ammo (3 Gunpowder + 1 Refined Ingot per shell), so reserve the shotgun for close-range burst situations where the Assault Rifle's per-shot damage isn't enough. The shotgun is particularly effective against Marcus and Faleris, who frequently close to melee range.

6. Advanced Bow — The Ammo-Free Endgame Option

Unlocked at Level 57, the Advanced Bow is the ultimate bow weapon. With 700 ATK at Legendary and a DPS of 1,900 (faster than most firearms), it offers incredible sustained damage without using gunpowder. Advanced Arrows only require Stone and Hexolite, making this the most ammo-efficient weapon in the game once you have a Hexolite supply chain.

The Advanced Bow is perfect for players who want to minimize resource spending on ammo while still maintaining top-tier DPS. It's also the best weapon for extended Pal catching sessions, where you need to weaken many Pals without burning through expensive firearm ammo.

🗡️ Best Melee Weapons

Melee weapons don't need ammo, making them useful for resource-conscious players. Here are the only ones worth crafting.

Melee combat in Palworld is a secondary option — ranged weapons dominate the game. However, melee weapons have two distinct advantages: they consume no ammo, and they can be used indefinitely without resource costs. This makes them valuable for extended exploration trips and as backup weapons when ammo runs dry.

1. Sword — The Best Melee Weapon

The Sword is the only melee weapon with a full Common → Legendary progression, going from 360 ATK (Common) to 540 ATK (Legendary). Unlocked at Level 44, it requires Refined Ingots and is crafted at the Weapon Workbench. While its raw damage is lower than most firearms, the Sword has the highest melee DPS in the game and a fast swing animation.

The Sword is best used as a backup weapon when you run out of ammo during a boss fight, or for clearing weak Pals without wasting bullets. It's also the go-to weapon for players who enjoy a more hands-on combat style.

Crafting cost: 20 Wood, 20 Stone, 30 Refined Ingot at a Weapon Workbench.

2. Beam Sword — The Endgame Energy Blade

Unlocked at Level 57, the Beam Sword is a Hexolite-tier melee weapon with 2,000 ATK — nearly four times the Sword's damage. However, it requires rare Hexolite and Nightstar Sand to craft, making it a luxury option for endgame players. The Beam Sword has a unique energy blade visual effect and a wider swing arc than the standard Sword.

3. Katana — The Mid-Game Blade

The Katana unlocks at Level 29 and offers 525 ATK — comparable to the Legendary Sword — but at a single rarity tier. It's a solid mid-game melee option if you prefer blade weapons, but it gets replaced by the Sword once you reach Level 44 and can craft the full rarity progression.

4. Refined Metal Spear — The Reach Option

The Refined Metal Spear (Level 34) offers 310 ATK with a longer reach than swords, making it useful for keeping distance from aggressive Pals. However, its damage falls off quickly in the late game. Use it as an early melee option before the Sword becomes available.

Melee Combat Tip: Melee weapons consume stamina with each swing. If you're fighting a tower boss with melee, manage your stamina carefully — you need it for dodging. Never commit to a full melee combo unless the boss is stunned or between attack phases. For most players, melee should be a backup option, not a primary strategy.

💣 Best Throwables & Grenades

Grenades are consumable burst-damage tools that don't take up a weapon slot. All grenade types deal 750 ATK, so choose by status effect.

Grenade ATK Effect Unlock Lv Best Use
Frag Grenade 750 Pure damage Lv 25 Boss fight openers
Shock Grenade 750 Paralyze (stun) Lv 27 Crowd control / stunning alphas
Ice Grenade 750 Slow movement Lv 29 Kiting fast Pals
Incendiary Grenade 750 Burn (DOT) Lv 31 Extra damage over time
Frag Grenade Mk2 4,000 Massive pure damage Lv 53 Endgame boss burst

Grenades are often overlooked, but they're one of the most cost-effective damage sources in the game. All standard grenades deal 750 ATK — more than a Common Musket shot — and they stack to 50 in your inventory. A single Frag Grenade costs only 1 Gunpowder, 10 Fiber, and 10 Stone to craft, making them far cheaper per-damage than firearm ammo.

The boss-fight grenade strategy: Open every tower boss and alpha Pal fight by throwing 2-3 Frag Grenades. This chunks the boss's HP by 10-20% before you even fire your weapon. If the boss summons adds or is particularly fast, swap to Shock Grenades to stun-lock them. This strategy works for all 5 tower bosses and is especially effective against Zoe and Grizzbolt, who are vulnerable during their opening animation.

For endgame content, the Frag Grenade Mk2 (Level 53) is a massive upgrade, dealing 4,000 ATK per throw — over 5 times the standard Frag Grenade. While more expensive to craft (requires Plasteel and Crude Oil), a stack of 20 Mk2 grenades can nearly solo some alpha Pals on their own.

Prioritize weapons over armor in the early game — a dead Pal doesn't need armor, but a dead player can't fight. Always keep 5 points in reserve for the next major weapon tier.

🔧 Ammo Types & Efficiency

Understanding ammo costs is critical to sustaining your combat effectiveness. Here's every ammo type ranked by damage-per-resource efficiency.

Ammo Type Unlock Lv Weapons Crafting Cost Efficiency Rating
Arrow Lv 3 Old Bow, Three Shot Bow 1 Wood + 1 Stone Excellent
Coarse Ammo Lv 21 Musket, Makeshift Handgun 1 Gunpowder + 1 Ingot Excellent
Handgun Ammo Lv 28 Handgun 1 Gunpowder + 1 Ingot Good
Rifle Ammo Lv 36 Single-Shot Rifle, Semi-Auto Rifle, Assault Rifle 2 Gunpowder + 1 Refined Ingot Good
Shotgun Shells Lv 39 All Shotguns 3 Gunpowder + 1 Refined Ingot Fair
Rocket Ammo Lv 49 Rocket Launcher 5 Gunpowder + 1 Pal Metal Ingot Expensive
Energy Cartridge Lv 51 Laser Rifle, Laser Gatling Gun 1 Electric Organ Fair
Missile Ammo Lv 55 Guided Missile Launcher 1 Pal Fluid + 1 Pal Metal Ingot + 1 Circuit Board + 1 Crude Oil Expensive
Advanced Arrow Lv 57 Advanced Bow 10 Stone + 1 Hexolite Excellent
Charge Rifle Ammo Lv 65 Charge Rifle 10 Electric Organ + 1 Hexolite + 1 Coralum Ingot Expensive

Ammo efficiency is the hidden stat that determines your real combat sustainability. A weapon with high damage but expensive ammo (like the Rocket Launcher) can only be used for short bursts, while a weapon with moderate damage and cheap ammo (like the Assault Rifle) can be fired continuously for extended sessions.

Best ammo-efficient loadout: Crossbow with arrows for early game, Musket with Coarse Ammo for mid-game burst, and Assault Rifle with Rifle Ammo for late-game sustained DPS. This combination gives you the best damage-per-resource ratio at every stage of the game. For truly ammo-free sustained damage, craft the Advanced Bow at Level 57 — its arrows only need Stone and Hexolite, no gunpowder at all.

Gunpowder production tip: Gunpowder is the bottleneck for all firearm ammo. Set up a dedicated Gunpowder production line at your base using a base location with nearby Sulfur and Charcoal resources. Assign Pals with Kindling suitability to automate Gunpowder crafting. A well-optimized base can produce 200+ Gunpowder per hour, enough to sustain continuous Assault Rifle use.

Buying ammo from merchants: The Small Settlement merchant (near the starting area) sells Coarse Ammo and Handgun Ammo for gold coins. If you have excess gold from selling items, buying ammo is faster than crafting. However, Rifle Ammo and higher-tier ammo types are not sold by merchants — you must craft them yourself.

📚 How to Get Legendary Schematics

Legendary (Tier 4) schematics boost your weapon's attack by up to 75%. Here's where to find them.

In Palworld, crafting a weapon at its base tier produces a Common rarity item. To craft higher rarity tiers (Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary), you need the corresponding Schematic in your inventory when you interact with the workbench. Each schematic is a single-use unlock — once consumed, the recipe is permanently available.

Where to find weapon schematics:

  • Tower Boss Rematches — After defeating a tower boss for the first time, you can rematch them for a chance to drop weapon schematics. The 5 tower bosses each have different schematic drop pools.
  • Alpha Pal Encounters — High-level alpha Pals (Level 40+) have a small chance to drop schematics when defeated. The rarer the alpha, the better the schematic tier.
  • Dungeon Chests — Random dungeons throughout the map contain chests with schematic drops. Higher-level dungeons have better drop rates.
  • Oil Rig Raids — The Sakurajima update added oil rig locations that can be raided for high-tier loot, including Legendary schematics. These are endgame content requiring Level 50+ gear.
  • Cursed Tyrant Blazamut — This special boss encounter has the highest drop rate for Legendary weapon schematics in the game. It's located in the endgame volcanic region.
  • Black Market Merchants — Some black marketeers sell schematics for gold coins. The inventory rotates, so check back regularly.

Schematic tier progression:

  • Schematic Lv 1 (Uncommon) — ~10-15% ATK boost. Drops from common enemies and low-level dungeons.
  • Schematic Lv 2 (Rare) — ~25-30% ATK boost. Drops from alpha Pals and mid-level dungeons.
  • Schematic Lv 3 (Epic) — ~45-50% ATK boost. Drops from tower bosses and high-level dungeons.
  • Schematic Lv 4 (Legendary) — ~60-75% ATK boost. Drops from endgame bosses, oil rig raids, and Cursed Tyrant encounters.

For example, an Assault Rifle goes from 320 ATK (Common) to 560 ATK (Legendary) — a 75% increase. This makes farming Legendary schematics one of the most impactful upgrades in the game, often more important than leveling up your Pal's passives. Check our breeding guide for how to breed combat Pals that complement your Legendary-geared arsenal.

Farming Tip: The fastest way to get Legendary schematics is to repeatedly rematch the tower boss that drops the schematic you need. Each rematch takes 3-5 minutes with a good loadout, and the drop rate for Epic/Legendary schematics is approximately 5-10% per kill. Bring a fast flying mount to quickly travel between boss arenas.

⚔️ Best Weapons for Tower Bosses

Each tower boss has different weaknesses and fight mechanics. Here's the recommended weapon loadout for each one.

Tower Boss Primary Weapon Secondary Weapon Grenades Strategy
Zoe & Grizzbolt Crossbow / Musket Makeshift Handgun Frag x3 Open with grenades, then sustained Crossbow fire. Dodge Grizzbolt's body slam.
Lily & Lyleen Musket / Single-Shot Rifle Makeshift Handgun Incendiary x3 Burn DOT compensates for Lyleen's healing. Keep distance.
Axel & Orserk Single-Shot Rifle Semi-Auto Rifle Shock x3 Stun-lock Orserk to prevent Electric AOE. Watch for lightning adds.
Marcus & Faleris Pump-Action Shotgun Assault Rifle Frag x5 Faleris closes to melee — shotgun burst at close range, AR at range.
Victor & Shadowbeak Assault Rifle Rocket Launcher Frag Mk2 x5 Open with Rocket Launcher for 14K burst, then AR sustained DPS. Hardest fight.

The 5 tower bosses each require a different approach. Zoe and Grizzbolt (the first boss) can be defeated with just a Crossbow and Frag Grenades. By the time you reach Victor and Shadowbeak (the final boss), you'll need a full endgame loadout with a Legendary Assault Rifle, Rocket Launcher, and Mk2 Grenades.

Universal boss-fight tip: Always open the fight with your highest burst-damage weapon (Rocket Launcher or Musket) to chunk the boss's HP before they start their attack patterns. Then switch to your sustained DPS weapon (Assault Rifle or Single-Shot Rifle) for the bulk of the fight. Save grenades for phase transitions — when the boss pauses between attack patterns, throw 2-3 grenades for free damage.

Pal synergy: Your weapon damage is amplified by your active Pal's passives. Anubis provides a player attack buff, Faleris boosts Fire damage, and Beakon boosts Electric damage. For detailed Pal recommendations, see our Best Pals tier list and Tower Boss strategy guide.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best weapon in Palworld?

The Rocket Launcher has the highest raw damage at 14,000 attack on Legendary tier, but it has slow reloads and expensive ammo. For sustained damage, the Assault Rifle is the best all-around weapon with 560 Legendary attack, high fire rate, and relatively cheap ammo. The Charge Rifle (Lv 65) is the ultimate endgame weapon with 12,500 attack per shot.

What level do you unlock guns in Palworld?

The first firearm is the Musket at Technology Level 21, using Coarse Ammo. The Makeshift Handgun follows at Level 24, and the Handgun at Level 28. The Assault Rifle unlocks at Level 45, and the Rocket Launcher at Level 49. All guns require a Weapon Workbench or higher to craft.

What is the best early game weapon in Palworld?

The Crossbow (Level 13) is the best early-game weapon, offering 280 attack with cheap arrow ammo. It outclasses all bows and carries you through the first two tower bosses. Upgrade to the Makeshift Handgun (Level 24) or Musket (Level 21) once you have Ingots to spare.

How do you get Legendary weapons in Palworld?

Legendary weapon schematics (Tier 4) drop from tower boss rematches, end-game dungeons, oil rig chests, and Cursed Tyrant boss encounters. Once you have a Legendary schematic in your inventory, the highest-rarity recipe unlocks at your Weapon Assembly Line, boosting the weapon's attack by 40-75% over the Common tier.

Do Pal weapons replace regular weapons in Palworld?

No. Pal partner skills like Tanzee's Assault Rifle or Pengullet's Rocket Launcher are supplementary burst attacks on cooldowns. They complement your crafted weapons but cannot replace sustained player DPS. Always pair a Tier 3+ crafted weapon with a damage-buff Pal like Anubis or Faleris.

What ammo does the Assault Rifle use in Palworld?

The Assault Rifle uses Rifle Ammo, unlocked at Level 36. Each round costs 2 Gunpowder and 1 Refined Ingot to craft. Rifle Ammo is also used by the Single-Shot Rifle and Semi-Auto Rifle, making it one of the most versatile ammo types in the game.

Is the Rocket Launcher worth crafting in Palworld?

Yes, absolutely. The Rocket Launcher deals 14,000 ATK at Legendary tier — the highest single-hit damage of any weapon. While the ammo is expensive (5 Gunpowder + 1 Pal Metal Ingot per rocket), you only need 2-3 rockets per boss fight as an opener. The burst damage is unmatched and can chunk 15-20% of a boss's HP instantly.

Should I use bows or guns in Palworld?

Use bows (specifically the Crossbow) from Level 13 to Level 21, then transition to guns. Bows have the advantage of not requiring gunpowder, making them extremely ammo-efficient. However, once you unlock firearms, the damage difference is enormous — a Musket deals 2,000 ATK per shot versus a Crossbow's 490. In the endgame, the Advanced Bow (Lv 57) becomes viable again as an ammo-free option with 700 ATK and 1,900 DPS.
