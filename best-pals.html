⚔ Top 8 Combat Pals — Detailed Analysis

Each Pal below has been extensively tested in tower boss fights, alpha Pal encounters, and PvP scenarios. We cover why they excel, where to catch them, and how to build them. Many of these top-tier Pals are most reliably obtained through breeding rather than wild captures. For the best weapons to pair with your combat Pals, see our complete weapon tier list.

1. Jetragon — The Ultimate Aerial Destroyer

Jetragon is the single most dominant combat Pal in Palworld, and it's not even close. As a Dragon-type Legendary, it boasts the highest base stats in the game and a partner skill that turns you into a flying missile platform. When mounted, you can fire homing missiles that track enemies and deal massive explosive damage, all while hovering safely out of reach of most ground-based attacks. Jetragon is also the fastest flying mount in the game, making travel a breeze. You will find it at the far western edge of the map in the Mount Obsidian volcanic region. It spawns as a level 50 Alpha, so bring your best gear and plenty of Legendary Spheres. For passives, prioritize Musclehead, Ferocious, and Lucky to max out its damage.

2. Frostallion — The Ice Legend

Frostallion is the Legendary Ice-type Pal and one of the most reliable DPS options for endgame content. Its signature active skill, Crystal Breath, unleashes a continuous beam of ice that shreds health bars and applies crowd control through slowing effects. Because Ice is super effective against Dragon types, Frostallion is your go-to counter for Dragon-type bosses including Jetragon encounters and the Victor & Shadowbeak tower fight. You can also ride Frostallion as a mount, and its partner skill modifies your attacks to deal bonus Ice damage. Frostallion roams the Land of Absolute Zero in the far north of the map. Prepare for a cold journey and bring cold-resistant armor.

3. Necromus — The Dark Lance Burst Caster

Necromus is one of the two Legendary Pals found in the Dessicated Desert in the northeast. It is a Dark-type with a devastating partner skill that summons dual energy lances for a high-damage burst combo. What makes Necromus special is its combination of raw burst potential and mobility — it functions as a fast ground mount while still being a top-tier combatant. Its Dark typing gives it an edge against Neutral-type enemies, and it has strong resistances that keep it alive in prolonged fights. Catch both Necromus and Paladius in the same trip since they spawn near each other.

4. Paladius — The Unbreakable Shield

Paladius is the second Legendary in the Dessicated Desert and serves as the tanky counterpart to Necromus. As a Ground-type, it has excellent defensive stats and a partner skill that grants you a shield while mounted, absorbing incoming damage. This makes Paladius ideal for players who prefer a methodical, survivable approach to combat rather than the glass-cannon playstyle of Jetragon or Necromus. Paladius excels in extended fights where attrition matters, such as tower boss runs where you need to survive the full ten-minute timer. Pair it with a high-DPS active skill and you have a well-rounded bruiser.

5. Blazamut — The Fire Powerhouse

Blazamut is the premier Fire-type combat Pal, found deep in the Scorching Mines on the far southwestern coast. Its partner skill grants a significant boost to Fire-type attacks while mounted, and its active skill roster includes some of the hardest-hitting Fire moves in the game. Blazamut is particularly strong against Grass and Ice types, making it a top pick for the Lily & Lyleen tower boss and for exploring the frozen northern regions. It is also one of the better-looking mounts in the game, which is a nice bonus. Be warned: the Scorching Mines are dangerous, so bring heat-resistant gear.

6. Jormuntide Ignis — The Dual-Type Hybrid

Jormuntide Ignis is the Ignis variant of Jormuntide and one of the rarest dual-type Pals, being both Dragon and Fire. This gives it incredible offensive coverage — it can hit most enemies for at least neutral damage and has no shortage of super-effective matchups. Beyond combat, Jormuntide Ignis is the only Pal in the game with Kindling Lv4, making it irreplaceable for base operations. You can find it on Wildlife Sanctuary No. 2, but it is a rare spawn. Alternatively, you can breed it by combining Blazamut with Jormuntide. This dual-purpose nature — elite combatant and essential base worker — makes Jormuntide Ignis one of the most valuable Pals to invest in.

7. Anubis — The Early-Mid Game MVP

Anubis is the best Ground-type combat Pal you can acquire without reaching the endgame. Its partner skill increases attack speed while using Ground-type moves, and its active skill Sand Tornado deals consistent damage over a wide area. Anubis is fast, hits hard, and is available much earlier than the Legendaries. You can find it in the Twilight Dunes region or, better yet, breed it using Penking and Bushi — one of the most efficient breeding combos in the game. Anubis is also the best base worker thanks to Handiwork Lv4, so even after you outgrow it in combat, it remains essential.

8. Shadowbeak — The Divine Disaster Wielder

Shadowbeak is a Dark-type Pal found on Wildlife Sanctuary No. 3 and is famous for its signature active skill, Divine Disaster. This skill creates a massive area-of-effect blast that chunks even the toughest enemies. Shadowbeak is also a flying mount, giving it excellent utility for both combat and exploration. Its Dark typing makes it strong against Neutral-type Alpha Pals, and it is a solid pick for the Victor & Shadowbeak tower fight since the boss version demonstrates exactly how powerful Divine Disaster can be. Breed Shadowbeak from Kitsun and Astegon if you cannot find it in the wild.

⚙ Top 8 Base Work Pals — Detailed Analysis

Each Pal below represents the pinnacle of its respective work category. Having all eight in a single base gives you full Lv4 coverage across every task in the game.

1. Anubis — The King of Crafting

Anubis is the single most important Pal for any base. It is the only Pal with Handiwork Lv4, which means it crafts items, weapons, and spheres faster than anything else in the game. On top of that, it has Mining Lv3, letting it double as an ore miner when crafting queues are empty. Anubis also has Transporting Lv2, so it moves raw materials to storage without your intervention. The best part? You do not need to reach the endgame to get one. Breed Penking with Bushi at a Breeding Farm and you can have an Anubis by level 19. Every base should have at least one, ideally two.

2. Jormuntide Ignis — The Only Kindling Lv4

Jormuntide Ignis is the sole Pal in the entire game with Kindling Lv4. This makes it irreplaceable for smelting ore into ingots, cooking food, and processing raw materials that require heat. Without it, your smelting speed is permanently capped at Lv3, which is significantly slower at scale. Jormuntide Ignis also doubles as a powerful Dragon/Fire combatant, so it earns its slot even outside of work duties. Find it on Wildlife Sanctuary No. 2 or breed it from Blazamut and Jormuntide. If you are running a production-heavy base, this Pal is non-negotiable.

3. Jormuntide — The Watering Specialist

Jormuntide is the Water/Dragon counterpart to Jormuntide Ignis and the only Pal with Watering Lv4. It is essential for irrigating crop farms, powering water mills, and operating stone crushers at maximum efficiency. If you have a Berry Plantation or Wheat Farm, Jormuntide will keep it watered around the clock without any input from you. It can be found in a locked dungeon on the west coast, but breeding is often more reliable. Pair Jormuntide with Lyleen for a complete farming loop — Lyleen plants the seeds, and Jormuntide waters them.

4. Orserk — The Power Generator

Orserk is the only Pal with Electricity Lv4, making it the backbone of any base that relies on powered equipment. Generators are required for refrigerators, assembly lines, and advanced crafting stations, and Orserk keeps them running at peak output. It also has Handiwork Lv2 and Transporting Lv3, so it fills in as a crafter and transporter when the generator is full. Find Orserk on Wildlife Sanctuary No. 3 or breed it from Grizzbolt and Relaxaurus. If your base keeps losing power, Orserk is the fix.

5. Astegon — The Ore Devourer

Astegon is the premier mining Pal with Mining Lv4. It tears through ore nodes, stone deposits, and coal veins faster than any other Pal in the game. If your base is positioned near ore spawns — and it should be — Astegon will keep your smelters fed with raw material non-stop. It is a Dark/Dragon type, which also makes it useful for nighttime defense against raids. You can find Astegon in the Scorching Mines or breed it from Helzephyr and Cryolinx. Pair it with Wumpo Botan for a mining-to-storage pipeline that never stalls.

6. Lyleen — The Farming Queen

Lyleen is the best farming Pal thanks to Planting Lv4 combined with Medicine Lv3 and Handiwork Lv3. It plants seeds at maximum speed, ensuring your farms are always productive, and it also crafts medicine to keep your other Pals healthy. This dual role makes Lyleen incredibly slot-efficient — one Pal covering both farming and healthcare. Lyleen is found on Wildlife Sanctuary No. 3, or you can breed it. For an optimized farming base, pair Lyleen with Jormuntide for watering and Wumpo Botan for transporting the harvest to storage.

7. Frostallion — The Cooling Engine

Frostallion is the only Pal with Cooling Lv4, which is essential for running refrigerators and cooling units at full capacity. Without a Cooling Lv4 Pal, your food and ingredients will spoil faster and your refrigeration efficiency drops significantly. Frostallion also happens to be one of the best combat Pals in the game, so it can pull double duty. Found in the Land of Absolute Zero in the far north, it requires cold-weather gear to reach. If you are running a cooking-focused base or need to preserve large quantities of food, Frostallion is mandatory.

8. Wumpo Botan — The Transport Champion

Wumpo Botan is the Botan variant of Wumpo and the best transporter in the game with Transporting Lv4. It moves items from production stations to storage boxes at an unmatched pace, ensuring your base never bottlenecks on logistics. It also has Lumbering Lv3 and Planting Lv1, giving it secondary utility in forestry and farming. Without a dedicated transporter, your crafting Pals will spend time walking items to storage instead of crafting. Wumpo Botan eliminates this problem entirely. Find it in snowy mountain regions or breed it from Wumpo and a Grass-type Pal.

Pro Tip: Passive skills matter enormously for base Pals. Always breed for Artisan (+50% work speed), Serious (+20% work speed), and Work Slave (+30% work speed, -20% attack) on your workers. These three stack multiplicatively, turning a fast worker into a blur. Avoid Vanguard and Musclehead on base Pals — their attack bonuses are wasted on non-combat roles.

📊 How We Ranked These Pals

Our tier list is based on hundreds of hours of hands-on testing across multiple save files and game versions.

For combat Pals, we evaluated four factors: raw DPS output measured against level 50 Alpha targets, survivability in tower boss encounters, partner skill utility including mount capabilities and special effects, and acquisition difficulty — a Pal that requires endgame spheres to catch is ranked with that context in mind.

For base work Pals, we evaluated: work suitability level (Lv4 is always top priority), versatility across multiple work types, autonomy — how well the Pal operates without babysitting — and synergy with other top-tier workers in a full base composition.

Tiers are assigned as follows: S Tier Pals are game-changers — basically must-haves if you want to play optimally. A Tier Pals are excellent and highly recommended, with minor caveats such as acquisition difficulty or slightly lower stats. Pals not listed here still work fine for casual play, but get outclassed when you're min-maxing.
