📊 How XP Works in Palworld

Understanding the XP system before you start grinding. Player XP, Pal XP, and the multipliers that matter.

Leveling Route Map

Approximate positions on the Palpagos Islands. Numbers match the list below.

1 Phase 1: Start — Lv 1-15, catch bonuses & starting area
2 Phase 2: Expand — Lv 15-30, alpha routes + Zoe loops
3 Phase 3: Push — Lv 30-45, Axel loops + alpha routes
4 Phase 4: Endgame — Lv 45-55+, Marcus/Victor loops

In Palworld, your character level is the gate to everything — weapons, armor, base structures, and mounts. Each level grants 1 Technology Point (sometimes 2 after Level 10), which you spend on the Tech Tree to unlock new recipes. Without enough levels, you're locked out of the game's best gear.

Player XP vs Pal XP: Your character and your Pals have separate XP bars with different requirements. Player XP requirements are much higher — reaching Level 50 requires 86,400 XP, while a Pal needs only 40,304 XP for the same level. However, Pals in your active party receive a share of all XP you earn, so they level alongside you automatically.

The level-scaling bonus: Pals that are significantly lower level than the player receive up to 3.6x bonus XP. This means a Level 5 Pal in a Level 40 player's party will rocket through levels. Use this to quickly catch up newly caught or bred Pals by bringing them along on high-level activities.

XP from enemy level: The XP you gain from killing a Pal scales with the enemy's level, not your own. A Level 10 enemy gives 39 XP whether you're Level 10 or Level 50. This means you should always fight the highest-level enemies you can handle to maximize XP per hour.

Key Takeaway: Always fight the highest-level enemies you can survive. The exponential XP scaling means that 5 kills at Level 35 are worth more than 100 kills at Level 15. Bring healing items, wear your best armor, and push your limits.

🎳 The Catch Bonus System — Your #1 XP Engine

The single most important XP mechanic in Palworld. Master this and you'll never struggle with levels again.

Every Pal species in your Paldeck has a Catch Bonus track. The first time you catch a species, you get a small XP bonus. The second catch gives a slightly bigger bonus. This escalates all the way to the 12th catch. Here's the full breakdown:

Catch #Bonus XPCatch #Bonus XPCatch #Bonus XP
1st15th69th38
2nd26th810th40
3rd47th1011th45
4th58th1312th47

Total bonus XP per species (12 catches): 148 XP — on top of the base XP from each catch.

That might not sound like much for a single species, but consider the math at scale: there are 180+ Pal species in Palworld. If you complete the 12/12 catch bonus for all of them, that's 26,640 bonus XP from catches alone — enough to reach approximately Level 48 before counting any combat, crafting, or exploration XP.

The Catch Bonus system is designed to reward exploration. Each biome has different Pal species, so the optimal strategy is to move to a new area, catch 10-12 of every species you find, then move on. By the time you've circled the map, you'll be Level 40+ without any repetitive grinding.

How to maximize catch bonus XP:

  1. Always carry 50+ Pal Spheres. You should never pass a wild Pal without catching it if you haven't completed its catch bonus. Check your Paldeck frequently to see which species still have bonuses available.
  2. Weaken before throwing. A Pal at low HP is much easier to catch. Use your weakest weapon (like the Old Bow) to chip HP without killing. One well-timed sphere on a weakened Pal saves 3-4 wasted throws.
  3. Use the right sphere tier. Pal Spheres work for Levels 1-15. Mega Spheres for Level 15-25. Giga Spheres for Level 25-35. Ultra Spheres for Level 35+. Hyper Spheres for Level 45+. Using a too-weak sphere on a high-level Pal wastes resources.
  4. Hit new biomes early. Even if enemies are 10 levels above you, you can catch them by sneaking up and throwing spheres. The catch bonus XP is the same regardless of the Pal's level. Just bring lots of spheres and accept some failed catches.
  5. Set up a Vixy (Caprity) ranch. Vixy (called Caprity in some regions) passively digs up Pal Spheres when assigned to a Ranch. This gives you a free, infinite supply of basic spheres for catch bonus farming. One Vixy produces approximately 10-15 spheres per hour.
Catch Bonus Math: Completing 12/12 catches on just 20 species gives 2,960 bonus XP — roughly 5-6 free levels in the early game. Prioritize species-dense areas like the starting plateau (Lamball, Cattiva, Chikipi, Lifmunk, Foxparks) where you can complete multiple catch bonuses in one session.

🐚 Alpha Pal Farming Routes

Alpha Pals give 10x kill XP and respawn every in-game day. Here's how to build efficient farming routes.

Alpha Pals are oversized boss versions of regular Pals that spawn at fixed locations on the map. They're significantly tougher than their normal counterparts, but the payoff is enormous: every Alpha Pal kill gives 10x the normal XP for that level. A Level 25 Alpha Pal gives 3,190 XP — more than 10 regular Pal kills at the same level.

Alpha Pals respawn one in-game day after being defeated (approximately 30 minutes of real-time). This makes them renewable farms. The key to efficient alpha farming is building a route — a sequence of alpha spawns that you clear in order, with enough travel time between them for earlier ones to respawn.

🎯 Tower Boss XP Loops

Tower bosses give 30x normal kill XP and can be refought infinitely. This is the fastest XP method in the mid-to-late game.

After you defeat a tower boss for the first time, you can re-enter the tower and fight them again immediately — no waiting required. Each rematch gives the same XP as the first kill, and you can skip the cutscene. Tower bosses give 30x the normal kill XP for their level, making them the highest XP-per-minute activity in the game.

The numbers speak for themselves. A single Victor and Shadowbeak kill gives 227,910 XP — more than 2.5 full levels at Level 50. Even accounting for the difficulty of the fight, the XP-per-hour from tower boss loops is unmatched by any other method.

However, tower boss loops require a significant gear investment. You need:

  • A Legendary-tier weapon (Assault Rifle minimum, Rocket Launcher preferred) — see our weapon guide for crafting details
  • Full armor appropriate for the boss's level
  • A party of 5 combat-optimized Pals — check our Best Pals tier list for recommendations
  • 50+ ammo and 10+ healing items per loop
  • Combat food buffs (Mammorest Curry for +25% Attack)

Best tower boss to loop by level:

  • Levels 10-20: Loop Zoe & Grizzbolt. Easy fight, fast clear, decent XP for early levels.
  • Levels 25-35: Loop Lily & Lyleen. She heals, so bring Incendiary Grenades for burn DOT. ~9,570 XP per kill.
  • Levels 40-50: Loop Axel & Orserk. 63,990 XP per kill — this is where tower loops become absurdly efficient. Watch for Orserk's Electric AOE and bring Shock Grenades.
  • Levels 50-55: Loop Marcus & Faleris or Victor & Shadowbeak. The XP is massive but the fights are hard. Use the tower boss strategy guide for specific tactics.
Tower Loop Optimization: Build a small base right next to each tower with a bed, feed box, and ammo crafting station. This lets you heal, restock ammo, and resume looping without traveling back to your main base. A fast flying mount like Vanwyrm or Beakon also cuts travel time between your main base and the tower.

🛑 Passive XP — Leveling While You Sleep

Your base generates XP 24/7 through crafting, mining, and farming. Optimize it for free overnight levels.

Every item crafted at your base — whether by you or by your Pals — grants a small amount of XP. This includes Pal Spheres, ingots, gunpowder, cloth, arrows, and food. While the per-item XP is small (typically 1-5 XP), a well-optimized base with 10+ working Pals can produce hundreds of items per hour, adding up to significant passive XP.

Best passive XP production chains:

Production ChainItems/HourEst. XP/HourSetup Level

Ingot smelting (Ore at furnace)~60~200Lv 10
Gunpowder production (Charcoal + Sulfur)~80~400Lv 21
Cloth production (Wool at textile mill)~50~200Lv 15
Berry farming + cooking~200~300Lv 5
Arrow production (Wood + Stone)~150~400Lv 3

A base running all of these chains simultaneously can generate 1,500–3,000 passive XP per hour. Over a 10-hour overnight session while you sleep, that's 15,000–30,000 free XP — roughly 2-4 levels at Level 30, or 1 level at Level 45.

How to build a passive XP base:

  1. Choose a resource-rich location. You need Ore, Stone, Wood, and Sulfur within your base radius. See our base location guide for the best spots.
  2. Assign Pals by suitability. Mining Pals (Digtoise, Reptyro) to Ore nodes. Kindling Pals (Foxparks, Arsox) to furnaces. Handiwork Pals (Anubis, Verdash) to workbenches. Transporting Pals (Cattiva, Lamball) to move materials between stations.
  3. Set up a Ranch with Vixy (Caprity). Vixy passively produces Pal Spheres, giving you free spheres for catch bonus farming AND generating crafting XP at the same time.
  4. Build multiple production lines. Don't just do one thing. Have a furnace line, a workbench line, a cooking line, and a ranch all running simultaneously. More production = more XP.
  5. Use Jam-Filled Buns for worker food. They provide high nutrition (60 per bun) so Pals eat less frequently, reducing downtime. See the food section below for details.
  6. Monitor SAN. Low-sanity Pals work slower and may get sick. Place Salad in the Feed Box to restore SAN, and ensure every Pal has a bed.

Don't forget: Your Pals also gain XP from base work, so a passive XP base doubles as a Pal leveling base. Pals assigned to your base will level up alongside you, even if they're not in your active party.

🍲 Food Buffs for Faster Leveling

Food doesn't give direct XP, but the right combat and work-speed buffs dramatically increase your XP-per-hour. Here's what to cook.

While no food in Palworld directly increases XP gain, the right food buffs make you kill faster, survive longer, and produce more — all of which translate to higher XP-per-hour. Think of food as a multiplier on your existing XP methods.

FoodBuffDurationBest For
Mammorest Curry +25% Attack 10 min Tower boss loops, alpha farming
Mozzarina Hamburger +20% Defense, +15% Attack 10 min All-around combat survival
Fried Chikipi +15% Work Speed 8 min Crafting XP farming, base work
Eikthyrdeer Stew +10% Attack 8 min Early-game combat (cheap ingredients)
Pancake +10% Movement Speed 5 min Exploration, traveling between alpha spawns

The combat food combo:

Different food buffs stack — so eating both Mammorest Curry (+25% Attack) and Mozzarina Hamburger (+20% Defense) gives you both buffs simultaneously. This is the optimal combat food combo for tower boss loops and high-level alpha farming. The combined +25% Attack and +20% Defense lets you kill bosses 25% faster and survive 20% longer, directly increasing your XP-per-hour by approximately 20-30%.

Important: Eating a second of the same food type does not stack — it just resets the timer. Always eat two different buff foods to get the stacking effect.

Food production for leveling:

  • Early game (Lv 1-15): Cook Fried Chikipi (Chikipi Poultry + Oil) at a Campfire for work speed buffs during crafting. Cook Eikthyrdeer Stew (Eikthyrdeer Venison + Mushrooms) for early combat.
  • Mid game (Lv 15-30): Build a Cooking Pot. Set up a Ranch with Mozzarina (for Milk) and Chikipi (for Eggs). Cook Mozzarina Hamburgers and Jam-Filled Buns (for worker food).
  • Late game (Lv 30+): Build an Electric Kitchen. Hunt Mammorest for Mammorest Meat. Cook Mammorest Curry for boss fights. Maintain a breeding farm with Cake production (see our breeding guide).

Feed Box tip: Always put worker food (Jam-Filled Buns) in the first slot of the Feed Box — Pals eat from slot 1 first. Put combat food in slot 2 so you can grab it when heading out. Check the Feed Box daily, as hunger drains even when you're offline.

Food Stacking Math: A player eating Mammorest Curry + Mozzarina Hamburger kills a Level 50 Alpha Pal ~25% faster than an unbuffed player. Over a 1-hour farming session, that's 3-4 extra kills — an additional 227,910–303,880 XP. The food costs less than 10 minutes to cook. Always eat before farming.

🚀 Step-by-Step Level 1 to 55+ Progression Route

The optimal leveling path from your first Pal capture to endgame. Follow this route to reach max level efficiently.

Phase 1: The Starting Plateau (Level 1→15)

Time estimate: 2–3 hours

Your only goal here is catch bonus completion. The starting plateau is packed with low-level Pal species: Lamball, Cattiva, Chikipi, Lifmunk, and Foxparks. Catch 10-12 of every species you see. Don't fight — just catch. Each completed catch bonus gives you 148 XP plus the base catch XP, and you'll complete 5+ species in the first hour.

  • Craft Pal Spheres at the Primitive Workbench (Stone + Wood + Paldium Fragments)
  • Catch 10+ of: Lamball, Cattiva, Chikipi, Lifmunk, Foxparks, Teafant, Fuack
  • Unlock the Crossbow at Level 13 — see our weapon guide
  • Activate every Fast Travel point you find for bonus XP
  • Kill the Level 3 and Level 5 Alpha Pals on the plateau for quick XP spikes

By the end of Phase 1, you should be Level 15 with a Crossbow, 5+ completed catch bonuses, and all nearby Fast Travel points activated.

Phase 2: Expansion (Level 15→30)

Time estimate: 4–6 hours

Now you expand into new biomes to find new species for catch bonuses. Head east into the forest, south to the riverside, and northeast into the plains. Each new area has 5-10 new species.

  • Catch 10+ of every new species in each biome (forest: Mossanda, Tanzee, Robinquill; riverside: Teafant, Fuack, Celaray; plains: Univolt, Nitewing, Melpaca)
  • Build your first base with a Ranch and assign Vixy for free Pal Sphere production — see base locations
  • Start the early game alpha route (Section 4 above) for 5,000+ XP per loop
  • Defeat Zoe & Grizzbolt (first tower boss) at Level 10+ for 1,170 XP + 5 Ancient Technology Points
  • Loop Zoe & Grizzbolt 3-5 times for quick early levels
  • Craft the Musket (Level 21) and Makeshift Handgun (Level 24) for better combat

By the end of Phase 2, you should be Level 30 with 15+ completed catch bonuses, a Musket or Single-Shot Rifle, and a functional passive XP base.

Phase 3: The Big Push (Level 30→45)

Time estimate: 5–8 hours

This is where the game opens up. You now have enough gear to farm mid-level content efficiently. Focus on alpha routes + tower boss loops + catch bonus completion simultaneously.

  • Start the mid-game alpha route (Section 4) — 50,690 XP per loop
  • Defeat Lily & Lyleen (second tower boss, Level 25) for 9,570 XP per kill. Loop her 5-10 times.
  • Push into the volcanic and desert biomes for new species (catch 10+ of each)
  • Craft the Single-Shot Rifle (Level 36) and Semi-Auto Rifle (Level 41)
  • Set up Gunpowder production at your base for ammo sustainability
  • Begin breeding combat Pals — see our breeding guide for optimal pairings
  • Defeat Axel & Orserk (third tower boss, Level 40) for 63,990 XP per kill — this is a game-changer

Once you can loop Axel & Orserk (63,990 XP per ~5-minute kill), you'll gain 2-3 levels per hour. This is the fastest leveling phase in the game.

Phase 4: Endgame Grind (Level 45→55+)

Time estimate: 5–10 hours

The final push. You have endgame gear, high-level Pals, and access to the hardest content. XP requirements are steep, but so are the rewards.

  • Craft the Assault Rifle (Level 45) and Rocket Launcher (Level 49) — see weapon guide
  • Loop Marcus & Faleris (Level 45 tower boss) for 120,570 XP per kill — approximately 1 full level per kill at Level 45-48
  • Run the late-game alpha route once per day for 350,000+ XP
  • Defeat Victor & Shadowbeak (final tower boss, Level 50) for 227,910 XP per kill
  • Loop Victor for 2-3 levels per hour — this is the single most efficient XP method in the game
  • Complete remaining catch bonuses for any species you missed
  • Tackle Sakurajima update content (oil rig raids, new alphas) for additional XP and Legendary schematics

By following this route, a focused player can reach Level 55+ in approximately 20–30 hours of playtime. Players who optimize food buffs, tower loops, and passive base XP can do it even faster.

Leveling Speed Summary: Phase 1 (Lv 1-15): ~2-3 hrs via catch bonuses. Phase 2 (Lv 15-30): ~4-6 hrs via alpha routes + Zoe loops. Phase 3 (Lv 30-45): ~5-8 hrs via Axel loops + alpha routes. Phase 4 (Lv 45-55+): ~5-10 hrs via Marcus/Victor loops. Total: ~16-27 hours to max level.

🐾 How to Level Up Pals Fast

Pals have their own XP system. Here's how to bring new catches and bred Pals up to fighting shape quickly.

Your Pals level up through three mechanisms: party XP sharing, combat participation, and base work. Understanding how to leverage all three is the key to maintaining a strong Pal team at every level bracket.

1. Party XP Sharing (Passive)

Every Pal in your active party (up to 5) receives a share of all XP you earn — whether from catching, combat, or crafting. The share is split among party members, so a full party of 5 Pals each gets 20% of the XP. Pals that are much lower level than you receive up to 3.6x bonus XP, making it easy to catch up underleveled Pals.

Strategy: Keep one or two low-level Pals in your party while doing high-XP activities (like tower boss loops). They'll level up 3x faster than normal. This is the best way to level up newly bred Pals that start at Level 1.

2. Alpha Pal Combat (Active)

Alpha Pal kills give massive XP to your party Pals. A single Level 35 alpha kill (11,360 XP) can level a Level 20 Pal by 3-4 levels instantly. Build alpha farming routes that match your Pal's level range — don't bring a Level 5 Pal to a Level 45 alpha fight, as it'll die before gaining any XP.

3. Base Work (Passive)

Pals assigned to your base gain XP from working — mining, crafting, farming, transporting, and kindling. While the per-action XP is small, a Pal working 24/7 at a well-stocked base gains levels surprisingly fast. A Level 1 Pal assigned to a mining node can reach Level 10 in a single overnight session.

Strategy: Use your base as a Pal daycare. Assign newly bred or caught Pals to easy tasks (like transporting or farming) where they can't get hurt. Check on them daily to monitor their progress and SAN levels.

4. Pal Condensing (Alternative)

If you don't want to grind levels, you can use the Pal Condensation system at the Statue of Power. Sacrificing duplicate Pals of the same species upgrades your main Pal's stats without leveling. Each condensation tier adds permanent stat boosts. This is especially useful for top-tier Pals that you want to max out without traditional leveling. See our breeding guide for how to mass-produce duplicates for condensation.

Pal Leveling Shortcut: The 3.6x bonus XP for underleveled Pals is incredibly powerful. If you're Level 50 and bring a Level 1 Pal to a Victor & Shadowbeak loop (227,910 XP per kill), that Pal will shoot to Level 25+ in a single kill. Use this to instantly level up bred Pals with perfect passives.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest way to level up in Palworld?

The fastest way to level up is to catch 10 of every Pal species you encounter. The first 10 catches of each species give escalating bonus XP through the Catch Bonus system. After completing your catch bonuses, farm Alpha Pal bosses (which give 10x normal kill XP) and repeat Tower Boss encounters (which give 30x normal kill XP). Combining all three methods can take you from level 1 to 50 in under 20 hours.

How does the catch bonus work in Palworld?

Each Pal species has a catch bonus track in your Paldeck. The first catch gives 1 bonus XP, the second gives 2, the third gives 4, and so on up to the 12th catch giving 22 XP. Completing all 12 catches of a single species yields a total of 148 bonus XP on top of the base catch XP. At 5 catches, the bonus jumps 2 steps; at 10 catches, it jumps 3 steps. With 176 fully completed species (12/12 catches each), catch bonus alone can get you to level 60.

Do Alpha Pals give more XP in Palworld?

Yes. Alpha Pals give 10x the normal kill XP of a standard Pal at the same level. For example, a level 25 normal Pal gives 319 XP, but a level 25 Alpha Pal gives 3,190 XP. Alpha Pals respawn after one in-game day, making them renewable XP farms. Setting up a route to hit multiple alpha spawns in sequence is one of the most efficient active leveling methods.

Can you repeat tower bosses for XP in Palworld?

Yes. After defeating a tower boss for the first time, you can re-enter the tower and fight them again immediately. Tower bosses give 30x normal kill XP — Zoe and Grizzbolt (Lv 10) give 1,170 XP per kill, while Victor and Shadowbeak (Lv 50) give 227,910 XP per kill. You can skip the cutscene on repeat runs, making each loop take only 3-5 minutes with a good loadout.

How do you level up Pals fast in Palworld?

Pals in your active party receive a share of all XP you earn, so they level passively as you play. To speed up Pal leveling specifically, fight Alpha Pal bosses — your party Pals receive large XP chunks from each kill. Pals deployed at your base also gain XP from working (mining, crafting, farming). Pals that are much lower level than the player receive up to 3.6x bonus XP, making it easy to catch up underleveled Pals.

Does food boost XP in Palworld?

Food does not directly boost XP gain, but it indirectly accelerates leveling. Combat food like Mammorest Curry (+25% Attack) and Mozzarina Hamburger (+20% Defense, +15% Attack) let you kill enemies faster and survive longer, increasing your XP-per-hour. Work speed food like Fried Chikipi (+15% Work Speed) makes base Pals produce resources faster, which speeds up crafting XP and Pal Sphere production for more catching.

Can you change the XP rate in Palworld?

Yes, in single-player and custom servers, you can adjust the EXP rate multiplier from 1x to 20x in World Settings. Go to Start Game → Select World → Change World Settings → Custom Settings → adjust the EXP Rate slider. This is useful for players who want to skip the grind, but note that it disables some achievements and is not available on official dedicated servers.

What level should I be for each tower boss?

Recommended levels: Zoe & Grizzbolt (Lv 10) at Level 10-15. Lily & Lyleen (Lv 25) at Level 25-30. Axel & Orserk (Lv 40) at Level 38-42. Marcus & Faleris (Lv 45) at Level 43-47. Victor & Shadowbeak (Lv 50) at Level 48-52. See our tower boss guide for detailed strategies and recommended loadouts for each fight.
